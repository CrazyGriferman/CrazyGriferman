托福疑难词汇 list 1～3

发表于 2020-01-20 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

List 1

Abbreviate v.

Meaning

缩短

Example

‘Chief Executive Officer’ is abbreviated as ‘CEO’.

Abolition n.

Meaning

废除

Example

The abolition of slavery in Brazil and the Caribbean closely followed the pattern of the United States.

Abort n.

Meaning

终止计划

Example

There was a general abort when the initial was discovered.

Abound v.

Meaning

充满

Example

The state abounds in colonial architecture.

Abrasion n.

Meaning

表面磨损

Example

Abrasion due to daily wear alters the surface features of beads.

Absurd adj.

Meaning

可笑的

Example

You want Granny to live in the caravan? That’s an absurd suggestion!

Acclaim v.

Meaning

称赞

Example

Her latest play has been widely acclaimed by critics.

Accomplished adj.

Meaning

多才多艺的

Example

They were accomplished musicians.

Acoustic adj.

Meaning

听觉的

Example

I don’t think the most ardent supporters of electronic instruments expect them to completely replace acoustic instruments.

Acting adj.

Meaning

代理的

Example

It was at the suggestion of her acting coach.

Acute adj.

Meaning

极度的

Example

With the acceleration of industrial growth came acute urban crowding.

Additive n.

Meaning

添加剂

Example

This margarine is full of additives - just look at the label.

Adhesive n.

Meaning

黏合剂

Example

You will need a strong adhesive to mend that chair.

Adjacent adj.

Meaning

邻近的

Example

Only in Pennsylvania and adjacent areas was stone widely used in dwellings.

Adjunct n.

Meaning

附加物

Example

The garage was an adjunct to the house.

Adobe n.

Meaning

泥砖

Example

If you visit my hometown, you can see a few blocks of adobe houses.

Adorn v.

Meaning

装饰

Example

Children adorned themselves with beads.

Advent n.

Meaning

到来

Example

With the advent of power-driven machinery, home industry began to give way to production in mills and factories.

Adverse adj.

Meaning

负面的

Example

The Group of Seven welcomed adverse criticism because it would help them improve as artists.

Aerial adj.

Meaning

空中的

Example

Meanwhile, the massive aerial bombing of military targets continued unabated.

Aesthetic adj.

Meaning

美学的

Example

Your artwork has no aesthetic value.

Affirm v.

Meaning

断言

Example

The scholar affirmed Shakespeare’s authorship of the plays.

Afflict v.

Meaning

使苦恼

Example

Financial difficulties afflicted the Smiths.

Aggregate n.

Meaning

聚集物

Example

Marine mammals have the misfortune to be swimming aggregates of commodities that humans want: fur, oil, and meat.

Airborne adj.

Meaning

空气传播的

Example

You increase your exposure to airborne viruses just when your body’s resistance is already low.

List 2

Alchemist n.

Meaning

炼金术士

Alga n.

Meaning

海藻

Alien adj.

Meaning

不同的

Example

Someone introduced an alien plant into the Florida swamp.

Alkali n.

Meaning

碱

Allay v.

Meaning

减轻

Example

Maria wrote stories interesting enough to attract children and morally instructive enough to allay adult distrust of fiction.

Allegory n.

Meaning

寓言

Example

Allegory is a literary device by which another level of meaning is concealed within what is usually a story.

Alloy n.

Meaning

合金

Altitude n.

Meaning

高度（海拔）

Example

Plants cannot move water to high altitudes.

Alumnus n.

Meaning

男校友

Example

We got the alumnus to make donations.

Amass v.

Meaning

积聚

Example

The shellfish amassed around the rocks in the shallow water.

Amino adj.

Meaning

氨基的

Ammonia n.

Meaning

氨

Amnesia n.

Meaning

健忘症

Example

Amnesia is usually not a permanent condition.

Ample adj.

Meaning

充足的

Example

The state of New Jersey offers ample opportunities for bicyclists of all abilities.

Amplify v.

Meaning

增强

Example

Can you amplify the sound so we can hear it better?

Analogy n.

Meaning

类比

Example

The teacher made an analogy between the lens of a camera and the lens of an eye.

Anatomy n.

Meaning

解剖

Example

We will discuss other features of the Neanderthal anatomy.

Antibiotic n.

Meaning

抗生素

Example

Now we have lots of antibiotics that kill bacteria.

Anticipate v.

Meaning

预见

Example

The test was harder than he had anticipated.

Antique n.

Meaning

古董

Example

You can’t give away Granny’s old bookcase - it’s a valuable antique.

Apprentice n.

Meaning

学徒

Example

The carpenter’s apprentice built this beautiful bookcase.

Appropriate v.

Meaning

占用

Example

The United States Congress appropriates some four million dollars a year for the upkeep of the White House grounds.

Aquarium n.

Meaning

水族馆

Aquatic adj.

Meaning

水生的

Example

The pond is quite small but can support many aquatic plants and fish.

Arbitrary adj.

Meaning

专断的

Example

The supervisor made an arbitrary decision to promote an employee.

Archaeology n.

Meaning

考古学

Example

Excavating human remains is one of the most exciting parts of archaeology.

Arid adj.

Meaning

干旱的

Example

Arid America’s soils are typically alkaline.

Artisan n.

Meaning

工匠

Ascending adj.

Meaning

上升的

Example

The architects laid out a system of public roads with stone staircases for ascending cliff faces.

List 3

Asteroid n.

Meaning

小行星

Asymmetrical adj.

Meaning

不均匀的

Example

Most people’s faces are asymmetrical.

Attorney n.

Meaning

辩护律师

Example

Bob went to law school and became an attorney.

Auction n.

Meaning

拍卖

Example

Auctions were another popular form of occasional
trade.

Audit v.

Meaning

旁听

Example

If you audit a course, you don’t have to take the tests.

Audition n.

Meaning

试唱

Example

He plans to sing a song at the audition.

Auditorium n.

Meaning

礼堂

Example

The Albert Hall is a huge auditorium.

Augment v.

Meaning

增大

Example

While searching for a way to augment the family income, she began making dolls.

Avalanche n.

Meaning

雪崩

Avert v.

Meaning

避免

Example

A fresh tragedy was narrowly averted yesterday.

Backlighing n.

Meaning

逆光

Bald adj.

Meaning

光秃的

Example

He’s bald and doesn’t need a haircut.

Banner n.

Meaning

横幅

Example

Thousands of people carried banners and flags.

Barb n.

Meaning

倒钩

Example

The head of the spear had a barb on it.

Bark n.

Meaning

树皮

Barn n.

Meaning

谷仓

Example

The escaped prisoners lay up in a barn for a few weeks, until the search had been called off.

Barrel n.

Meaning

桶

Example

Their customers drink a million barrels of beer a year.

Barren adj.

Meaning

贫瘠的

Example

We drove through a barren, rocky landscape.

Barter v.

Meaning

实物交易

Example

Food could be bartered for other commodities before.

Basin n.

Meaning

盆地

Bead n.

Meaning

珠子

Example

The Homestead Act of 1862 gave beads of families or individuals the right to own 160 acres of public land.

Beak n.

Meaning

鸟嘴

Example

Birds use their beaks to pick up food.

Beaver n.

Meaning

海狸

Benign adj.

Meaning

良好的

Example

The climate appears to have been fairly benign for a very long time.

Besiege v.

Meaning

包围

Example

Almost daily the public is besieged by claims for “no-aging” diets, new vitamins, and other wonder foods.

Bison n.

Meaning

美洲的野牛

Blade n.

Meaning

刀片

Example

He screamed as the blade sliced into his leg.

Bland adj.

Meaning

无味的

Example

This pizza tastes bland. There is not enough sauce on it.

Blast v.

Meaning

爆发

Example

Something about coal being set on fire blasted with a mixture of steam and oxygen.

Bleach v.

Meaning

漂白剂

Example

Raw cloth is bleached and then dyed.

Blip n.

Meaning

回落

Example

Analysts described the drop in share prices as a temporary blip.
