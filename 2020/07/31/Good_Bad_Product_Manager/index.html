关于产品经理的一篇好文章

发表于 2020-07-31 | 更新于 2020-08-01 | 分类于 reading | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

这篇文章是我在看《创业维艰》的时候读到的，里面的有些话很有启发性。

GOOD PRODUCT MANAGER/BAD PRODUCT MANAGER

Good product managers know the market, the product, the product line, and the competition extremely well and operate from a strong basis of knowledge and confidence. A good product manager is the CEO of the product. Good product managers take full responsibility and measure themselves in terms of the success of the product.

They are responsible for right product/right time and all that entails. A good product manager knows the context going in(the company, our revenue funding, competition, etc.), and they take responsibility for devising and executing a winning plan(no excuses).

Bad product managers have lots of excuses. Not enough funding, the engineering manager is an idiot, Microsoft has ten times as many engineers working on it, I’m overworked, I don’t get enough direction. Our CEO doesn’t make these kinds of excuses and neither should the CEO of a product.

Good product managers don’t get all of their time sucked up by the various organizations that must work together to deliver the right product at the right time. They don’t take all the product team minutes; they don’t project manager the various functions; they are not gofers(someone who does boring jobs for someone else) for engineering. They are not part of the product team; they manage the product team. Engineering teams don’t consider good product managers a “marketing resource”. Good product managers are the marketing counterparts to the engineering manager.

Good product managers crisply define the target, the “what”(as opposed to the “how”), and manage the delivery of the “what”. Bad product managers feel best about themselves when they figure out “how”. Good product managers communicate crisply to engineering in writing as well as verbally. Good product managers don’t give direction informally. Good product managers gather information informally.

Good product managers create collateral, FAQs, presentations, and white papers that can be leveraged by salespeople, marketing people, and executives. Bad product managers complain that they spend all day answering questions for the sales force and are swamped. Good product managers anticipate the serious product flaws and build real solutions. Bad product managers put out fires all day.

Good product managers take written positions on important issues(competitive silver bullets, tough architectural choices, tough product decisions, and markets to attack or yield). Bad product managers voice their opinions verbally and lament that the “powers that be” won’t let it happen. Once bad product managers fail, they point out that they predicted they would fail.

Good product managers focus the team on revenue and customers. Bad product managers focus the team on how many features competitors are building. Good product managers define good products that can be executed with a strong effort. Bad product managers define good products that can’t be executed or let engineering build whatever they want(that is, solve the hardest problem).

Good product managers think int terms of delivering superior value to the marketplace during product planning and achieving market share and revenue goals during the go-to-market phase. Bad product managers get very confused about the differences among delivering value, matching competitive features, pricing, and ubiquity. Good product managers decompose problems. Bad product managers combine all problems into one.

⭐️Good product managers think about the story they want written by the press. Bad product managers think about covering every feature and being absolutely technically accurate with the press. Good product managers ask the press questions. Bad product managers answer any press question. Good product managers assume members of the press and the analyst community are really smart. Bad product managers assume that journalists and analysts are dumb because they don’t understand the subtle nuances(a very slight difference) of their particular technology.

Good product managers err on(to do more than is really necessary) the side of clarity. Bad product managers never even explain the obvious. Good product managers define their job and their success. Bad product managers constantly want to be told what to do.

Good product managers send their status reports in on time every week, because they are disciplined. Bad product managers forget to send in their status reports on time, because they don’t value discipline.

感悟

我觉得这篇文章里面有些东西还是比较虚的，比如“product manager is the CEO of the product”，但是有些内容值得让人深思，特别是具体到某些事的时候，好的产品经理和差的产品经理的区别。

Good product managers don’t get all of their time sucked up by the various organizations that must work together to deliver the right product at the right time.

印象里产品经理是要和很多部门打交道的一个职位，那么产品经理如何分配这些“打交道”的时间？在学校的时候，可能一周有许多事要忙，要和很多人“打交道”，比如要完成小组的作业，有个比赛要弄啥的。当我们遇到两个小组作业冲突的时候，我们该如何解决？如果这些团队的任务一定需要在一起才嫩完成，我们又如何分配时间？说来惭愧，我现在在学校里就不能很好的分配时间，有时脑子里就常常会一直想着一件事情，导致其它事情就没有按时完成（甚至会影响到学业）。

Good product managers are the marketing counterparts to the engineering manager.

这句话我不是很明白，结合它的上一句，应该是说工程部的经理不会把一个好的产品经理当作是“marketing”的资源，而要当成与工程部经理相当的且具有marketing能力的人？

网上是这么解释的:

Product Managers should be operating more as part of the marketing department than in engineering.

也就是说，产品经理更偏重于marketing部门而非engineering部门，背后的意思是指要学习marketing部门的同学的思维来看待问题吗？

Good product managers crisply define the target, the “what”(as opposed to the “how”), and manage the delivery of the “what”.

这个crisply define 和 crisply communicate 和下面这句句子传达的含义是类似的：

Good product managers err on(to do more than is really necessary) the side of clarity.

无论是定义一个产品，还是和其它部门的人交流，都需要谈及细节，如果只是谈个大概，那么就容易产生模糊（二义性），这样不利于产品的研发等。一个好的产品经理需要关注到产品的细节，在提出需求的时候，一定要讲清细节。沟通也是这样，有时候如果不讲清细节，就会容易造成误会。就像这个暑假我跟着一个老师搞东西，一开头我没问清楚，结果一个星期过去我给她讲了思路之后，她说我理解错了， 要重新弄。所以，一定要过问细节啊…

Good product managers think about the story they want written by the press. Bad product managers think about covering every feature and being absolutely technically accurate with the press.

这句话我认为非常有意思。在公司内部讨论的时候，产品经理要谈“细节”，但是在对外的时候，好的产品经理不会谈这个产品里“各种各样”的功能，而是要谈到这个产品要解决的问题是什么，我们的愿景是什么！故事永远是吸引人的，在对外解释产品是干什么的是时候，好的产品经理往往会巧妙地构思这个故事。

Good product managers create collateral, FAQs, presentations, and white papers that can be leveraged by salespeople, marketing people, and executives.

Good product managers take written positions on important issues(competitive silver bullets, tough architectural choices, tough product decisions, and markets to attack or yield).

我觉得这两段想要传达的是类似的： 产品经理要干的事是什么？

写作能力、演说能力、沟通能力都是产品经理的“核心竞争力”。产品经理要会画产品经理（Axure），还要会写需求文档和竞品分析。这些都是基本能力，是需要我去学习的。
