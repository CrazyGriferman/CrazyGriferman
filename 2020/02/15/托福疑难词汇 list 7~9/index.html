托福疑难词汇 list 7～9

发表于 2020-02-15 | 更新于 2020-02-16 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

List 7

Confine v.

Meaning

限制

Example

I shall attempt to confine the discussion to broad principles.

Congenial adj.

Meaning

性格温和的

Example

He is back in more congenial company.

Congestion n.

Meaning

拥塞

Example

The project aims to reduce traffic congestion.

Consent n.

Meaning

同意

Example

He entered the building without the owner’s consent.

Conserve v.

Meaning

保存

Example

To conserve electricity, we are cutting down on our central heating.

Consonant adj.

Meaning

协调的

Example

The man is behaving with a dignity consonant with his rank.

Consort v.

Meaning

结交

Example

They claimed he had been consorting with drug dealers.

Constellation n.

Meaning

星座

Example

A planet is orbiting a star in the constellation of Cepheus.

Constituent n.

Meaning

成分

Example

The main constituents of wine are acid, alcohol and sugar.

Constitute v.

Meaning

被看作

Example

The long-term unemployed now constitute a sort of underclass.

Constitution n.

Meaning

宪法

Example

The US Constitution guarantees freedom of the press.

Constricted adj.

Meaning

狭窄的

Example

When asthmatic suffers an attack, the airway path was constricted, making it difficult for the person from breathing normally.

Contagious adj.

Meaning

传染性的

Example

A hobby like collection of classical music can be contagious and expensive.

Contaminate v.

Meaning

污染

Example

Industrial sewage continues to contaminate our beaches.

Continuum n.

Meaning

连续统一体

Example

It’s not ‘left-wing’ or ‘right-wing’ - political opinion is a long continuum.

Contour n.

Meaning

轮廓

Example

The sculpture reproduces the smooth contours of the human body.

Convection n.

Meaning

对流

Example

Warm air rises by the process of convection.

Convention n.

Meaning

惯例

Example

She rebelled against convention and refused to marry.

Converge v.

Meaning

聚合

Example

Top diplomats were converging on Washington from all over the world.

Coral n.

Meaning

珊瑚

Corona n.

Meaning

日冕

Corrosion n.

Meaning

腐蚀

Example

There was a lot of corrosion on the bottom of the car.

Corrosive adj.

Meaning

腐蚀的

Example

Sodium and sulphur are highly corrosive.

counseling n.

Meaning

咨询服务

Example

She was offered counseling after the attack

Counselor n.

Meaning

顾问

Example

He hired Jeffrys as his counselor.

Counteract v.

Meaning

消除

Example

Schools are taking action to counteract bullying.

Counterpart n.

Meaning

相对物

Example

The new model is vastly better than its 1997 counterpart.

Crab n.

Meaning

蟹

Example

We walked along the beach collecting small crabs.

List 8

Cramped adj.

Meaning

狭窄的

Example

We have six desks in this room, so we’re rather cramped.

Crater n.

Meaning

坑

Example

With a good telescope, you can see craters on the moon.

Credential n.

Meaning

证明书

Example

All the candidates had excellent academic credentials.

Crest n.

Meaning

顶部

Example

Small craters have rim crests that are elevated above the surrounding terrain.

Crippling adj.

Meaning

造成严重损害的

Example

A crippling attack of malaria kept him in bed for months.

Crisscross v.

Meaning

交叉往来

Example

Coaches and wagons continued to crisscross the West.

Critique n.

Meaning

批评

Example

She had brought a book, a feminist critique of Victorian lady novelists.

Crusade n.

Meaning

运动

Example

They led an unsuccessful crusade against government corruption.

Crust n.

Meaning

硬层

Example

I washed the crust of make-up from my face.

Crustacean n.

Meaning

甲壳类动物

Cumbersome adj.

Meaning

笨重的

Example

Modern hand-held cameras are far less cumbersome.

Cuneiform adj.

Meaning

楔形的

Cylinder n.

Meaning

圆柱体

Example

It was recorded on a wax cylinder.

Dazzling adj.

Meaning

眼花缭乱的

Example

The view was dazzlingly beautiful.

Debris n.

Meaning

残骸

Example

Debris from the aircraft was scattered over a large area.

Debut n.

Meaning

首次演出

Example

Cooper made his debut at Covent Garden in 1989.

Deceptive adj.

Meaning

欺骗性的

Example

The hotel looked nice but appearances can be deceptive.

Deciduous adj.

Meaning

每年落叶的

Example

Deciduous trees lose all their leaves each autumn.

Decipher v.

Meaning

破译

Example

I always wonder how people manage to decipher my doctor’s handwriting.

Decode v.

Meaning

译解

Example

Decoding the paintings is not difficult once you know what the component parts symbolize.

Decompose v.

Meaning

分解

Example

The body must have been decomposing for several weeks.

Defect n.

Meaning

过失

Example

There are a few minor design defects.

Deficient adj.

Meaning

不足的

Example

A diet deficient in vitamin D may cause the disease rickets.

Deform v.

Meaning

使变形

Example

Wearing shoes that are too tight will deform your feet.

List 9

Delta n.

Meaning

三角洲

Example

the Nile delta

Democrat n.

Meaning

民主主义者

Example

This bill will be welcomed by democrats everywhere.

Demolish v.

Meaning

拆除

Example

Disused factories were being demolished, and the whole site redeveloped.

Denote v.

Meaning

表示

Example

Bad stitching denotes a poor quality.

Depict v.

Meaning

描写

Example

He was depicted as a hero who died for his beliefs.

Deplete v.

Meaning

使枯竭

Example

Wars in the region have depleted the country’s food supplies.

Designate v.

Meaning

指定

Example

Nobody has yet been designated as the leader.

Desolate adj.

Meaning

荒凉的

Example

The house stood in a bleak and desolate landscape.

Destined adj.

Meaning

注定的

Example

We both felt that we were destined to meet.

Deter v.

Meaning

阻止

Example

The rain didn’t deter people from coming to the game.

Detest v.

Meaning

憎恶

Example

I detest having to get up when it’s dark outside.

Devastate v.

Meaning

破坏

Example

Western India was devastated by a huge earthquake.

Devise v.

Meaning

发明

Example

They’ve devised a scheme to allow students to study part-time.

Devoid adj.

Meaning

全无的

Example

Rob’s face was devoid of any warmth.

Devour v.

Meaning

贪婪地吃

Example

He was already devouring detective fiction as a young child.

Diffuse v.

Meaning

传播

Example

Light gases diffuse through porous surfaces more rapidly than heavy gases.

Digestive adj.

Meaning

消化的

Example

Peppermint oil is very good for regulating digestive disorders.

Directory n.

Meaning

目录

Example

I couldn’t find your number in the telephone directory.

Discern v.

Meaning

洞悉

Example

Officials were keen to discern how much public support there was.

Discharge v.

Meaning

释放

Example

Hospitals now tend to discharge patients earlier than in the past.
