托福疑难词汇 list 10～12

发表于 2020-02-16 | 更新于 2020-02-20 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

List 10

Dismal adj.

Meaning

阴沉的

Example

My prospects of returning to a suitable job are dismal.

Dismay v.

Meaning

使气馁

Example

What dismayed me most about their behavior was their rudeness.

Displace v.

Meaning

取代

Example

Investment in the nationalized industries has simply displaced private investment.

Dispose v.

Meaning

使倾向于

Example

His rudeness when he first met didn’t dispose me very kindly towards him.

Dispute v.

Meaning

争论

Example

They don’t dispute the fact that the company is in trouble.

Disseminate v.

Meaning

散布

Example

One of the organization’s aims is to disseminate information about the disease.

Distort v.

Meaning

弄歪

Example

The paper was accused of distorting the truth.

Distress n.

Meaning

痛苦

Example

I wouldn’t want to cause her any distress.

Divert v.

Meaning

转移

Example

Police are trying to divert traffic away from the trouble spot.

Doctrine n.

Meaning

教条

Example

The president said he would not go against sound military doctrine.

Dormant adj.

Meaning

静止的

Example

The long-dormant volcano has recently shown signs of erupting.

Downtown adj.

Meaning

在市区的

Example

He works in an office tower in downtown San Francisco.

Drastic adj.

Meaning

剧烈的

Example

The company will be taking drastic measures to reduce its debt.

Dubious adj.

Meaning

可疑的

Example

We were dubious about signing the deal.

Ductile adj.

Meaning

易延展的

Example

Ductile metals can be pressed or pulled into shape without needing to be heated.

Dwarf v.

Meaning

使······显得矮小

Example

Our small investment has been dwarfed by what the private sector has put in.

Dwelling n.

Meaning

住处

Example

There is an estimated shortfall of some five million dwellings across the country.

Eccentric adj.

Meaning

古怪的

Example

She’s regarded as being rather eccentric.

Edible adj.

Meaning

可食用的

Example

Only the leaves of the plant are edible.

Edifice n.

Meaning

大厦

Example

The whole edifice of EU environmental policy is threatened by this bill.

Eject v.

Meaning

逐出

Example

A group of noisy protesters were ejected from the meeting.

Elicit v.

Meaning

得出

Example

The question elicited a positive response from 60% of voters.

Elliptical adj.

Meaning

椭圆的

Example

Kepler published his discovery of the elliptical orbits of planets in 1609.

Elm n.

Meaning

榆树

Eloquent adj.

Meaning

雄辩的

Example

The pictures were an eloquent reminder of the power of the volcano.

List 11

Elusive adj.

Meaning

难懂的

Example

The answers to these questions remain as elusive as ever.

Embellish v.

Meaning

修饰

Example

He couldn’t resist embellishing the story of his accident a little.

Emblem n.

Meaning

徽章

Example

A rose is the national emblem of England.

Embody v.

Meaning

体现

Example

The spirit of hope is embodied in the character Anna.

Embryo n.

Meaning

胚胎

Example

Between the eighth week of development and birth a human embryo is called a foetus.

Emerald n.

Meaning

绿宝石

Eminent adj.

Meaning

显著的

Example

He is one of Britain’s most eminent scientists.

Enactment n.

Meaning

制定

Example

We support the call for the enactment of a Bill of Rights.

Encase v.

Meaning

包住

Example

The nuclear waste is encased in concrete before being sent for storage in disused mines.

Encompass v.

Meaning

包含

Example

The student debates will encompass a range of subjects.

Encroach v.

Meaning

侵占

Example

The federal government is encroaching on a state issue.

Endeavor v.

Meaning

努力

Example

Engineers are endeavoring to locate the source of the problem.

Endow v.

Meaning

资助

Example

The ambassador has endowed a $1 million public-service fellowships program.

Engraving n.

Meaning

雕刻术

Engulf v.

Meaning

吞没

Example

Several workers died in the landslide that engulfed the old factory.

Entail v.

Meaning

牵涉

Example

The cuts will entail some job losses.

Envelop v.

Meaning

包围

Example

Black smoke enveloped the square.

Enzyme n.

Meaning

酶

Example

An enzyme in the saliva of the mouth starts the process of breaking down the food.

Ephemeral adj.

Meaning

短暂的

Example

Fame in the world of rock and pop is largely ephemeral.

Epitomize v.

Meaning

成为···的缩影

Example

Singapore’s Changi airport epitomizes the state’s efficiency.

Epoch n.

Meaning

时代

Example

The president said that his country was moving into a new epoch which would be one of lasting peace.

Equator n.

Meaning

赤道

Erect v.

Meaning

建立

Example

A memorial to her was erected after her death.

Erode v.

Meaning

侵蚀

Example

It is feared that international institutions may erode national sovereignty.

Errand n.

Meaning

差事

Example

She’s out running errands for her mother.

Erupt v.

Meaning

爆发

Example

The room erupted in laughter as Sally acted out the incident.

Eternal adj.

Meaning

永恒的

Example

She has earned our eternal gratitude.

Ethics n.

Meaning

道德规范

Example

He said he was bound by a scientist’s code of ethics.

Evacuation n.

Meaning

撤离

Example

The evacuation of civilians remains out of the question while the fighting continues.

Evaporate v.

Meaning

蒸发

Example

The high concentration of sugars forms a syrup when the sap evaporates.

Exalted adj.

Meaning

崇高的

Example

She rose to the exalted post of Foreign Secretary.

Excavate v.

Meaning

挖掘

Example

In tin mining today, workers excavate tunnels horizontally from a vertical shaft.

List 12

Exemplary adj.

Meaning

模范的

Example

His tact was exemplary, especially considering the circumstances.

Exempt adj.

Meaning

被免除的

Example

Currently, developing nations are exempt from certain restrictions on carbon emissions.

Exert v.

Meaning

发出

Example

A well-funded national organization would be able to exert more influence in Parliament.

Exhale v.

Meaning

呼出

Example

Take a deep breath in then exhale into the mouthpiece.

Exhilarating adj.

Meaning

令人高兴的

Example

It was exhilarating to be on the road again and his spirits rose.

Exotic adj.

Meaning

外来的

Example

They’re always flying off to film in exotic locations.

Expedition n.

Meaning

远征

Example

We are hoping to mount the first manned expedition to Mars by 2020.

Expel v.

Meaning

排出

Example

When you breathe out, you expel air from your lungs.

Expire v.

Meaning

到期

Example

The loan offer is due to expire this week.

Exponent n.

Meaning

拥护者

Example

Adam Smith was an exponent of free trade.

Extol v.

Meaning

赞美

Example

The report extolled the virtues of Internet technology.

Extravagant adj.

Meaning

过度的

Example

He made extravagant claims on his son’t behalf.

Exuberant adj.

Meaning

充满活力的

Example

Young and exuberant, he symbolizes Italy’s new vitality.

Fabric n.

Meaning

结构

Example

Drug abuse poses a major threat to the fabric of society.

Fabricate v.

Meaning

编造

Example

The defense accused police of fabricating evidence.

Facade n.

Meaning

表面

Example

All that jollity is just a facade.

Fahrenheit n.

Meaning

华氏温度计

Example

The temperature was 72 degrees Fahrenheit.

Fatigue n.

Meaning

劳累

Example

A child’s sleep problems cause parents fatigue and unnecessary guilt.

Faucet n.

Meaning

水龙头

Example

She turned off the faucet and dried her hands.

Fauna n.

Meaning

动物群

Example

Brackish waters generally support only a small range of faunas.

Feat n.

Meaning

功绩

Example

The Eiffel Tower is a remarkable feat of engineering.

Feeble adj.

Meaning

虚弱的

Example

He made a feeble attempt to start a conversation with me.

Feminist n.

Meaning

女权主义者

Example

All her life she was an ardent feminist.

Ferment v.

Meaning

使发酵

Example

You make wine by leaving grape juice to ferment until all the sugar has turned to alcohol.

Feudal adj.

Meaning

封建的

Example

the feudal system

Fiber n.

Meaning

纤维

Example

Natural fibers such as cotton tend to be cooler.
