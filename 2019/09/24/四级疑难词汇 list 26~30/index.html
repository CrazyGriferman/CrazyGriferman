四级疑难词汇 list 26~30

发表于 2019-09-24 | 更新于 2019-09-25 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

List 26

Presumably adv.

Meaning

大概

Example

They are students, so presumably they won’t have a lot of money.

Prevail v.

Meaning

流行

Example

The prevailing theory seems to be that we’re all hot-tempered single mothers who can’t keep a man.

Preventive adj.

Meaning

预防性的

Priest n.

Meaning

神父

Example

He led the campaign for women to become Anglican priests.

Privilege n.

Meaning

特权

Example

The manager told me I can enjoy all the benefits and privileges of club membership.

Proclaim v.

Meaning

宣告

Example

The country asserts again to use force against the nearby island if it tries to proclaim independence.

Proficient adj.

Meaning

熟练的

Example

Mike is proficient in English and French.

Prominent adj.

Meaning

突出的

Example

The order was given by a prominent member of the government.

Prompt v.

Meaning

促使

Example

Curiosity prompted Cecelia to ask the professor a few questions.

Pronoun n.

Meaning

代词

Prose n.

Meaning

散文

Example

She writes beautiful prose.

Prosperous adj.

Meaning

繁荣的

Example

Compared with the place where I grew up, this town is more prosperous and exciting.

Province n.

Meaning

省

Provoke v.

Meaning

引起

Example

The new published book provoked a heated discussion in mathematics.

Publicity n.

Meaning

宣传

Example

Her behavior during the filming attracted a lot of free publicity.

Pulse n.

Meaning

脉搏

punctual adj.

Meaning

准时的

Example

Michael’s always very punctual for appointments.

Quota n.

Meaning

限额

Example

The government is planning to introduce quotas on sugar production.

Rack v.

Meaning

使······痛苦

Example

Losing love racked her with pain.

List 27

Rag n

Meaning

破布

Example

The little girl was dressed in rags.

Raid n.

Meaning

(突然)袭击

Example

The rebels carried out a surprise raid on the military camp yesterday.

Rail v.

Meaning

责骂

Example

He established a forum to rail against medicare and social security.

Rally v.

Meaning

重新振作

Example

Robin will rally soon, and be happy with others.

Ratio n.

Meaning

比率

Realm n.

Meaning

范围

Example

This is not really within the realms of my experience.

Reap v.

Meaning

获得

Example

We will all reap the benefits of this important research.

Rebel v.

Meaning

反叛

Example

The forces rebelled against the government.

Receiver n.

Meaning

听筒

Example

I went to pick up the receiver, but it had stopped ringing.

Recorder n.

Meaning

录像机

Example

Rodney put the recorder on the desk top and pushed the play button.

Recruit v.

Meaning

招募（新兵）

Example

Dr. Seaborg was recruited into the Manhattan Project.

Refine v.

Meaning

精炼

Example

Crude oil can be refined into various petroleum products.

Refrain v.

Meaning

抑制

Example

Please refrain from smoking during dinner.

Relay v.

Meaning

转播

Example

Rose immediately relayed news of the accident to her boss.

Remedy v.

Meaning

补救

Example

He wished to remedy his mistake and please you.

List 28

Render v.

Meaning

给予

Example

A lot of people were rendered jobless by the financial crisis.

Renovate v.

Meaning

翻新

Example

The buildings are being renovated into 41 apartments, mainly for families earning less than $30,000 a year.

Reproduce v.

Meaning

繁殖

Example

An individual worker bee cannot reproduce itself.

Republican n.

Meaning

共和党人

Reservoir n.

Meaning

(知识、人才等的)储藏

Example

The universities constitute a reservoir of expert knowledge.

Resort v.

Meaning

求助

Example

His punishing work schedule had made him resort to drugs.

Restless adj.

Meaning

焦躁不安的

Example

She noticed that several students seemed restless that morning.

Restrain v.

Meaning

阻止

Example

I don’t know how to restrain her tears.

Resume n.

Meaning

简历

Example

This is a nice and impressive resume.

Retain v.

Meaning

保留

Example

It will be important to retain independence to work effectively in an interdependent society.

Retreat v.

Meaning

The army was forced to retreat back down the mountain on account of darkness.

Revenue n.

Meaning

收入

Example

I promise the invention will bring in considerable revenue for our family.

Rhythm n.

Meaning

节奏

Rib n.

Meaning

肋骨

Ribbon n.

Meaning

丝带

Example

The river appeared as a ribbon of blue between the mountains.

Riddle n.

Meaning

谜

Example

No one could provide an answer to the riddle of Elise’s death.

Ridge n.

Meaning

脊

Example

The cottage is set on a ridge in the Chiltern Hills.

Rifle n.

Meaning

来复枪

Rigid adj.

Meaning

严格的

Example

Several colleges in our study have rigid rules about student conduct.

Riot n.

Meaning

暴乱

Example

Sixteen people are now known killed in the race riot.

Ripe adj.

Meaning

熟的

Example

Those bananas aren’t ripe yet - they’re still green.

Rod n.

Meaning

杆

Example

He was given a fishing rod for his birthday.

Roller n.

Meaning

滚筒

Example

As the hot metal passed between the huge rollers it was pressed into thin sheets.

Rotate v.

Meaning

（使）旋转

Example

They rotate their positions and exchange their points of view.

List 29

Rug n.

Meaning

（小）地毯

Example

He sat sipping his tea with a rug over his knees.

Rumor n.

Meaning

谣言

Example

I’ve heard a rumor that the company is making a new film.

Runway n.

Meaning

飞机跑道

Rust n.

Meaning

铁锈

Example

Before painting, remove all traces of rust with a wire brush.

Sack v.

Meaning

洗劫

Example

The enemies sacked our city after their invasion.

Saddle n.

Meaning

马鞍

Example

He has been in the saddle for 20 years.

Saint n.

Meaning

圣徒

Sanction v.

Meaning

批准

Example

If the king wants a second marriage, he has to get the church’s sanction.

Satellite n.

Meaning

卫星

Sauce n.

Meaning

调味汁

Scandal n.

Meaning

丑事

Example

The company has been damaged by a series of financial scandals.

Scar n.

Meaning

伤疤

Example

He has a scar under his left eye.

Scatter v.

Meaning

散开

Example

Rats scatter into the shadows ahead of her.

Scent n.

Meaning

香味

Example

The small garden was filled with the scent of fresh flowers.

Scope n.

Meaning

范围

Example

The new law is limited in scope.

Scout n.

Meaning

侦查员

Example

I’ll have a scout round the village.

Scrape v.

Meaning

刮

Example

You’ll have to scrape the walls before you paint them.

Screw n.

Meaning

螺丝（钉）

Example

The teacher put the screw on us that we must hand in our papers this weekend.

Script n.

Meaning

剧本

Example

I have two questions about guarding this original script.

Sector n.

Meaning

部门

Example

They tend to work as managers, especially in the small business sector.

Segment n.

Meaning

部分

Example

The straight lines divided the special area into several segments.

Seminar n.

Meaning

研讨会

Example

I attended practically every lecture and seminar when I was a student.

Senate n.

Meaning

上院

Setback n.

Meaning

挫折

Example

Falling share prices may be another setback for the troubled economy.

Shave v.

Meaning

刮脸

Example

After getting up, I shave and brush my teeth.

Shed v.

Meaning

脱落

Example

The autumn is coming and some trees are beginning to shed their leaves.

Sheer adj.

Meaning

十足的

Example

We were overwhelmed by the sheer volume of work.

List 30

Shield n.

Meaning

盾

Example

My relationship with my sister was a shield against the ugliness of our childhood.

Shiver n.

Meaning

战栗

Example

A cool breeze from the doorway makes her shiver.

Shrug n.

Meaning

耸肩

Example

“Fine, I give you space.” Asher said with a shrug and then walked away.

Sideways adv.

Meaning

从一边（的）

Example

I slipped on the ice and fell sideways.

Siege n.

Meaning

包围

Example

The troops prepared to withstand a long siege.

Singular adj.

Meaning

独一无二的

Example

The singular aim was to avoid a disaster.

Sip v.

Meaning

小口地喝

Example

She was already sitting at the bar, sipping wine.

Skyscraper n.

Meaning

摩天大楼

Slam v.

Meaning

砰地关上

Example

The doors slammed against Green and opened again.

Slap v.

Meaning

拍

Example

The coach slapped me on the back and said “Good job!”

Sleeve n.

Meaning

袖子

Example

You’d better roll your sleeves up or you’ll get them dirty.

Slender adj.

Meaning

细长的

Example

My price must be tall, slender, and dark.

Slope v.

Meaning

（使）倾斜

Example

Those trees slope down the hill.

Soak v.

Meaning

浸

Example

You can soak up enough vitamin if you eat this kind of food.

Sole adj.

Meaning

唯一的

Example

His sole motive was to make her happy.

Solemn adj.

Meaning

庄严的

Example

The adults gazed at us with solemn eyes.

Souvenir n.

Meaning

纪念品

Example

Remember buying a souvenir of Italy for me.

Sow v.

Meaning

播种

Example

Whatever a man sow, that shall he also reap.

Spark n.

Meaning

火花

Example

A little spark kindles a great fire.

Sparkle v.

Meaning

闪烁

Example

Bits of broken glass sparkled in the sunlight.
