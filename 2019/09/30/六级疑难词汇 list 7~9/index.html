List 7

Conform v.

Meaning

遵守

Example

Students can be expelled for refusing to conform to school rules.

Congregate v.

Meaning

聚集

Example

Crowds began to congregate to hear the President’s speech.

Conscientious adj.

Meaning

认真的

Example

A conscientious teacher may feel inclined to take work home.

Consecutive adj.

Meaning

连续的

Example

Can they win the title for the third consecutive season?

Consensus n.

Meaning

一致

Example

There is a consensus among teachers that children should have a broad understanding of the world.

Consent n.

Meaning

同意

Example

He took the car without the owner’s consent.

Consequent adj.

Meaning

随之发生的

Example

The rain and the consequent cancellation of the baseball game left us with nothing to do.

Conserve v.

Meaning

保护

Example

We must conserve our woodlands for future generations.

Console v.

Meaning

安慰

Example

Distraction is the only thing that consoles us for our miseries, and yet it is itself the greatest of our miseries.

Conspicuous adj.

Meaning

显眼的

Example

The notice must be displayed in a conspicuous place.

Conspire v.

Meaning

密谋

Example

There was some evidence that he had been conspiring against the government.

Constituent v.

Meaning

选民

Example

The senator worked hard, always talking to his constituents and hearing their problems.

Consulate n.

Meaning

领事馆

Example

Peter’s father used to be a consul worked in the American consulate in China.

Consumption n.

Meaning

消耗

Example

The government is determined to reduce the UK’s total energy consumption.

Contagious adj.

Meaning

接触传染的

Example

A hobby like collecting classical music can be contagious and expensive.

Contaminate v.

Meaning

污染

Example

Drinking water supplies are believed to have been contaminated.

Contemplate v.

Meaning

注视

Example

Tom contemplates their situation with concern.

Contempt n.

Meaning

轻视

Example

Peter can’t conceal his contempt for the beggar.

Contend v.

Meaning

声称

Example

They contend that the theory is right.

Contention n.

Meaning

论点

Example

Her main contention is that doctors should do more to encourage healthy eating.

Contingent adj.

Meaning

视情况而定的

Example

The accident is really a contingent event that we have not expected.

Continuity n.

Meaning

连续性

Example

We should ensure continuity of care between hospital and home.

Contradict v.

Meaning

反驳

Example

They will not contradict each other

Contradiction n.

Meaning

矛盾

Example

There is a contradiction between the government’s ideas and its actual policy.

Contrive v.

Meaning

谋划

Example

They had contrived a naughty plan of revenge.

Controversy n.

Meaning

争论

Example

The election ended in controversy, with allegations of widespread vote-rigging.

Convene v.

Meaning

开会

Example

The foreign policy advisers convened for an emergency session to settle the problem.

Convention n.

Meaning

习俗

Example

She rebelled against convention and refused to marry.

Converge v.

Meaning

聚集

Example

Several streets converge on the square.

Converse v.

Meaning

交谈

Example

Selina felt entirely appropriate to converse with advocacy organizations and constituent groups on this issue.

Conversion n.

Meaning

转变

Example

Her conversion to Buddhism was a very gradual process.

Convict v.

Meaning

定罪

Example

The jury convicted the man of fraud.

Convoy n.

Meaning

车队

Example

A convoy of trucks containing supplies was sent to the famine area.

Cooperative adj.

Meaning

合作的

Example

The suspect was cooperative with investigators.

Coral n.

Meaning

珊瑚

Cord n.

Meaning

细绳

Example

The robe was held at the waist by a cord.

Cordial adj.

Meaning

热诚的

Example

The letter you wrote to me is very cordial.

Cork n.

Meaning

软木塞

Example

Champagne corks popped as we celebrated our victory.

Corporal adj.

Meaning

身体的

Example

Corporal punishment is prohibited at school.

Corps n.

Meaning

队

Example

A corps of technicians is accompanying the band on their tour.

Corpse n.

Meaning

尸体

Example

Most of the corpses were badly burned, making identification almost impossible.

Corrode v.

Meaning

腐蚀

Example

Metal like iron corrodes easily.

Corrupt adj.

Meaning

腐败的

Example

Corrupt judges have taken millions of dollars in bribes.

Cosmopolitan adj.

Meaning

世界性的

Example

Port functions will make a city cosmopolitan.

Costume n.

Meaning

服装

Example

The dancers were in national costume.

Council n.

Meaning

理事会

Example

He sent a letter to the council to complain about the noise.

Counsel n.

Meaning

律师

Example

The judge asked counsel for the defence to explain.

After that, Tom and Robin decided to end their day dinning in a restaurant that served Chinese cuisine.

Example

Curtail v.

Meaning

Example

Custody n.

Meaning

Example

He got custody of his son after the divorce.

Customary adj.

Meaning

Example

In the pub, tip of 10 yuan seems to be customary.

Cylinder n.

Meaning

Meaning

Example

The public is cynical about election promises.

Dart v.

Meaning

Example

As the rain began to fall harder, the passersby all darted into the shop.

Dazzle v.

Meaning

Example

As children, we were dazzled by my uncle’s good looks and charm.

Deadlock n.

Meaning

Example

The talks have reached a complete deadlock.

Dean n.

Example

The is the new dean of the Faculty of Social Sciences.

Debris n.

Example

As the sliding ice and snow melted, it touched off devastating torrents of mud and debris, which destroyed all life in their path.

Debut n.

Example

Cooper made his debut at Covent Garden in 1989.

Decimal adj.

Meaning

Example

If you calculate the result to two decimal places, that should minimize any possible errors.

Decree v.

Example

Dedicate v.

Meaning

Example

He has dedicated his life to scientific research.

Deduct v.

Meaning

Example

The employer deducted income tax from the salary.

Deem v.

Meaning

认为

Example

Tom deemed himself a favored and happy man.

Default n.

Meaning

Example

The company is in default on its loan agreement.

Defendant n.

Meaning

被告

Example

The jury concluded from the evidence that the defendant was innocent.

Defer v.

Meaning

推迟

Example

Further discussion on this new project will be deferred.

Defiance n.

Meaning

违抗

Example

Many people were drinking in the streets, in defiance of the ban.

peace talks.

Example
