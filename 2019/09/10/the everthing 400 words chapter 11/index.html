《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Eleven

发表于 2019-09-10 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Negative First Impressions

Disheveled adj.

Meaning

untidy, rumpled

Example

The old woman’s disheveled appearance embarrassed her family.

Sloppiness n.

Meaning

untidiness

Example

Their son’s sloppiness angered his parents.

Slovenly adj.

Meaning

dressed neglectfully, carelessly

Example

The young waitress was repulsed by the man’s slovenly attire

Tawdry adj.

Meaning

gaudy and cheap in appearance

Example

Against her mother’s advice, Toni wore a tawdry dress to the prom.

Unkempt adj.

Meaning

uncombed; messy

Derived from

un- ➡️ not

kempt ➡️ well-combed, neat

Example

The man’s unkempt long hair and beard frightened the bank teller.

Negative Physical Appearances

Corpulent adj.

Meaning

obese; overweight

Derived from

corpus ➡️ body

-ulentus ➡️ full of

Example

The corpulent man could no longer fasten his coat.

Harridan n.

Meaning

a scolding, vicious woman

Example

Joan’s mother-in-law was a harridan who made Joan’s life miserable.

Repugnant adj.

Meaning

arousing strong feelings of dislike

Derived from

re- ➡️ back

pugnare ➡️ to fight

Example

Her landlord’s scowling face was so repugnant to Amy, she decided to find another apartment.

Ungainly adj.

Meaning

awkward; clumsy

Derived from

un- ➡️ not

gegn ➡️ convenient

Example

The tall, thin boy was ungainly, but everyone liked his cheerful smile.

Negative Personality Traits

Acrimonious adj.

Meaning

bitter; ill-natured

Example

His acrimonious attitude alienated his employees.

Mendacious adj.

Meaning

lying; untruthful

Example

Sue’s mendacious gossip left her friendless.

Pugnacious adj.

Meaning

quarrelsome; combative

Derived from

pugnare ➡️ to fight

Example

Tom’s pugnacious nature landed him in jail.

Salacious adj.

Meaning

lewd; dirty minded

Example

Offended by her date’s salacious remarks, Sally left the restaurant.

Sycophant n.

Meaning

An ambitious, insincere flatterer

Derived from

sykon ➡️ to fig

-phant ➡️ to show

Example

A skilled sycophant, Jones wooed and married a wealthy old widow.

Positive First Impressions

Fastidious adj.

Meaning

being careful about detail

Example

Nancy was so fastidious about her hair, her makeup, and her clothes that it took her an hour to get dressed.

Immaculate adj.

Meaning

spotless; perfectly clean

Derived from

in- ➡️ not

maculatus ➡️ spotted, defiled

Example

She wore immaculate white gloves to the ceremony.

Impeccable adj.

Meaning

flawless; virtually perfect

Derived from

in- ➡️ not

pecare ➡️ to sin

Example

Ken, a man of impeccable taste in clothing, served as an example to his colleagues.

Positive Physical Appearance

Adonis n.

Meaning

a Greek god, a symbol of masculine physical perfection

Example

Lucy called the good-looking lifeguard her Adonis.

Enchanting adj.

Meaning

Delightful; charming

Example

Ivy greeted him with an enchanting smile.

Lithesome adj.

Meaning

graceful; supple; pliant

Example

The ballerina’s lithesome movements charmed her audience.

Pulchritude n.

Meaning

beauty; a beautiful figure

Example

The marble statue captured the goddess’s feminine pulchritude.

Winsome adj.

Meaning

charming, in a childlike way; cheerful; attractive

Derived from

wynn ➡️ pleasure, delight

-sum ➡️ some

Example

The barmaid’s winsome appearance attracted the male customers.

Positive Personality Traits

Charisma n.

Meaning

a quality of leadership, confidence, and personal appeal

Example

The teacher’s charisma inspired her pupils.

Debonair adj.

Meaning

suave; polite; charming

Example

Paul’s debonair manner ensured his acceptance into high society.

Solicitous adj.

Meaning

anxious or concerned; eager to help; attentive to the needs of others

Example

A solicitous nurse was very kind to me during my illness.

Staunchness n.

Meaning

loyalty

Example

Janet endured her husband’s financial reverses with admirable staunchness.

Virtuous adj.

Meaning

Having high moral principles; honest

Example

I can trust Alice, a virtuous woman.
