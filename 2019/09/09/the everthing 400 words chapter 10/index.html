《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Ten

发表于 2019-09-09 | 更新于 2019-09-28 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Positive Action Words

Benevolence n.

Meaning

a desire to do kindness

Derived from

bene- ➡️ well

Example

Little Mary took to heart the story of the Good Samaritan’s benevolence.

Magnanimity n.

Meaning

generosity of mind, forgiving

Derived from

magnus ➡️ great

animus ➡️ mind, soul, spirit

Example

Anne’s magnanimity enabled her to forgive her uncle’s thoughtlessness in her time of need.

Philanthropy n.

Meaning

the love of humankind, shown through practical actions, gifts of money, and support of worthy causes

Derived from

phil- ➡️ loving

anthropos ➡️ mankind

Example

Her love of knowledge and philanthropy paid for the education of many deserving students.

Solicitous adj.

Meaning

concerned or anxious about something or someone

Example

Solicitous about the welfare of her old aunt, Kelly took time to visit her every day.

Negative Action Words

Iniquitous adj.

Meaning

extremely immoral or unfair

Derived from

in- ➡️ negative prefix

aequus ➡️ just, equal

-ous ➡️ adjective suffix

Example

It was iniquitous of Joan to spread false rumors about her rival at work.

Malicious adj.

Meaning

Resulting from hatred, ill-will, or a desire to harm

Derived from

mal- ➡️ bad

Example

Spray-painting her ex-husband’s SUV was a malicious thing to do.

Ruthless adj.

Meaning

cruel; having no compassion

Example

The ruthless cruelty of the invading barbarians terrified the population.

Transgression n.

Meaning

a violation of a rule; a sin

Derived from

trans- ➡️ across

gradi ➡️ to walk, go

Example

Max was tempted to cheat on the exam but he knew it would be a major transgression.

Vindictive adj.

Meaning

mean-spirited; eager for revenge

Example

In a vindictive fit, Lynn destroyed her sister’s homework.

Naughty Action Words

Frivolity n.

Meaning

Foolish, lighthearted, noisy, silly behavior

Example

A neighbor called the police when the young people’s frivolity on the beach became too noisy.

Impish adj.

Meaning

exhibiting playful, teasing, or joking behavior

Example

Kevin had an impish grin(broad smile) on his face when he drew a funny birthday card for his dad.

Mischievous adj.

Meaning

playful in a teasing way

Derived from

mischief

-ous ➡️ adjective suffix

Example

The mischievous twins Mindy and Lindy planned many April Fool’s jokes

Prankishness

Meaning

the act of playing pranks or tricks on people

Example

Eddie made people laugh, but eventually his prankishness began to annoy his roommate.

Happy Events

Bacchanal n.

Meaning

a drunken orgy

Example

Worshipers of Bacchus would attend a bacchanal

Centennial n.

Meaning

a hundredth anniversary

Derived from

cent ➡️ hundred

Example

The small town celebrated its centennial with a parade, dancing in the streets, and fireworks.

Commemoration n.

Meaning

a celebration in memory of a person or of an outstanding event

Derived from

com- ➡️ with, together

memorare ➡️ to remind

Example

In John’s family, the commemoration of independence Day was a big celebration.

Jubilee n.

Meaning

any season of rejoicing and festivity

Example

The king celebrated the fifteenth year of his monarchy with a nationwide jubilee.

Matriculation n. ⭐️

Meaning

admission into college

Example

Joe’s entire family was proud of his matriculation to the university.

Nuptials n.

Meaning

of or relating to a wedding

Example

Their nuptials were a solemn ceremony.

Sad Events

Disconsolate adj.

Meaning

unable to overcome sadness

Example

After Albert dies, the disconsolate Queen Victoria wore black for the rest of her life.

Doleful adj.

Meaning

sad; depressed

Example

When Ron lost his job, he became doleful and withdrawn.

Grievous adj.

Meaning

causing grief, distress, or severe injury

Derived from

grief

Example

When Mark fell off the cliff, he suffered grievous injuries.

Lamentation n.

Meaning

an expression of mourning or sorrow

Example

Julie’s lamentation over her broken love affair troubled her friends.

Disastrous Events

Calamity n.

Meaning

a destructive event that brings terrible loss

Example

The influenza epidemic was a worldwide calamity.

Catastrophe n.

Meaning

an enormous, destructive occurrence; a complete failure

Derived from

cata- ➡️ down

strephein ➡️ turn

Example

The tsunami that hit Indonesia and the coasts of other Asian countries was a catastrophe that will never be forgotten.

Conflagration n.

Meaning

a huge fire

Derived from

com- ➡️ with, together

flagrare ➡️ to burn

Example

The wildfire was a conflagration that burned thousands of acres.

Deluge n.

Meaning

a great flood

Derive from

dis- ➡️ away

-luere ➡️ to wash

Example

The biblical flood that Noah survived was a deluge.

Tempest n.

Meaning

a violent windstorm often accompanied by rain, hail, or snow

Example

A tempest left them shipwrecked on an uninhabited island.
