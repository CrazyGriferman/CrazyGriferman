四级疑难词汇 list 16~20

发表于 2019-09-22 | 更新于 2019-10-26 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

List 16

Goodness n.

Meaning

善良

Example

She acted out of the goodness of her heart.

Gorgeous adj.

Meaning

华丽的

Example

Eric’s website contains gorgeous photographs from his trip around the globe.

Gown n.

Meaning

礼服

Example

Those film stars are wearing beautiful gowns to the party.

Grain n.

Meaning

谷物

Gram n.

Meaning

克

Example

Asians on average eat 20 grams of soy per day.

Gravitation n.

Meaning

重力

Example

Gravitation keeps the moon orbiting round the earth.

Grind v.

Meaning

磨（碎）

Example

Store the peppercorns in an airtight container and grind the pepper as you need it.

Grip n.

Meaning

紧握

Example

The president maintains an iron grip on his country.

Grocer n.

Meaning

食品商

Gross adj.

Meaning

总的

Example

His gross income is $70 per week.

Gulf n.

Meaning

海湾

Example

The hurricane caused enormous devastation across the islands in the gulf.

Gut n.

Meaning

勇气

Example

The new Chancellor has the guts to push through unpopular tax increases.

Habitat n.

Meaning

栖息地

Example

The grassland is an important habitat for many wild flowers.

Halt v.

Meaning

使（停止）

Example

The construction of the new park was halted because of a financial problem.

Handicap n.

Meaning

（身体或智力方面的）缺陷

Example

Poor eyesight may not be a physical handicap to a student.

Harness n.

Meaning

马具

Example

He was not wearing a safety harness when he fell.

Harsh adj.

Meaning

严厉的

Example

The hostages are being held in harsh.

Haste n.

Meaning

急忙

Example

The more haste, the less speed.

Hatch v.

Meaning

孵化

Example

Eagle eggs usually hatch between late May and early June.

Haul v.

Meaning

使苦恼

Example

The memory of the horrible events still haunts the girl.

Hazard n.

Meaning

危险

Example

My coworkers have been worrying about health conditions and complaining about safety hazards in the workplace.

Haze n.

Meaning

霾

Example

There was an early-morning haze across the valley.

List 17

Heartfelt adj.

Meaning

衷心的

Example

Please accept my heartfelt apologies.

Heave v.

Meaning

（用力）举起

Example

It took five strong men to heave it up a ramp and lower it into place.

Hectare n.

Meaning

公顷

Hedge n.

Meaning

篱笆

Example

She kicked the ball so powerfully that it flew over the hedge.

Heel n.

Meaning

脚后跟

Example

She got badly spiked when one of the runners trod on her heel.

Heir n.

Meaning

继承人

Example

Hesketh was the heir to a grocery fortune.

Herd n.

Meaning

放牧

Example

Every summer he worked on his uncle’s farm, herding sheep.

Hereby adv.

Meaning

以此

Example

I hereby sentence you for life after all the charges against you have been proven true.

Heroine n.

Meaning

女英雄

Example

This latest horror movie characterizes its heroine as capricious and passionate.

Hinder v.

Meaning

阻碍

Example

The career of the governor was hindered by the bribery scandal.

Hip n.

Meaning

臀部

Horn n.

Meaning

警报器

Example

He sounded the car horn.

Horsepower n.

Meaning

马力

Hospitable adj.

Meaning

好客的

Example

The Chinese have always enjoyed the reputation of being very hospitable in the world.

Hostile adj.

Meaning

不友善的

Example

People of that small town were said hostile towards outsiders.

Hut n.

Meaning

小屋

Example

Modern houses have replaced the one-room mud huts with grass roofs that had been home to generations of peasants.

Hyphen n.

Meaning

连字号

Identical adj.

Meaning

完全相同的

Example

All the plants were grown under identical conditions.

Idiom n.

Meaning

习语

Example

How many idioms have you learned today?

Idle v.

Meaning

虚度（光阴）

Example

I used to idle away the hours watching TV.

Immense adj.

Meaning

巨大的

Example

There is still an immense amount of work to be done this month.

List 18

Imperial adj.

Meaning

帝国的

Example

The building is a reminder of the country’s imperial past.

Implicit adj.

Meaning

含蓄的

Example

Ryan’s words could be understood as an implicit admission of guilt.

Impulse n.

Meaning

冲动

Example

He had an almost irresistible impulse to talk to the crowd when he entered Hyde Park.

Incentive n.

Meaning

鼓励

Example

Awards provide an incentive for young people to improve their skills.

Incline v.

Meaning

（使）倾向于

Example

We incline to the view that things are either totally good or totally bad.

Indispensable adj.

Meaning

必需的

Example

This book is an indispensable resource for researchers.

Induce v.

Meaning

诱使

Example

Nothing would induce me to take this job.

Inflation n.

Meaning

通货膨胀

Example

Adjusted for inflation, public funding for international exchanges and foreign-language study is well below the levels of 40 years ago.

Influenza n.

Meaning

流行性感冒

Infrastructure n.

Meaning

基础设施

Example

The war has badly damaged the country’s infrastructure.

Inland adj.

Meaning

内陆的

Example

The Black Sea is a large inland sea.

Inn n.

Meaning

小旅店

Insane adj.

Meaning

精神失常的

Example

For the last ten years of his life he was clinically insane.

Installment n.

Meaning

（分期付款的）一期付款

Example

The young couple paid for the house in installment.

Intact adj.

Meaning

完整无缺的

Example

Few factories remained intact after the earthquake.

Intake n.

Meaning

纳入

Example

Some of the new intake of the staff were good at computer operation.

List 19

Intangible adj.

Meaning

无形的

Example

She had that intangible quality that a player must possess to be a champion.

Integrity n.

Meaning

正直

Example

The chairman’s integrity is unquestioned.

Intensive adj.

Meaning

加强的

Interior adj.

Meaning

内部的

Example

My husband prefers the larger car with wide interior room.

Invaluable adj.

Meaning

无价的

Example

The new job would provide you with invaluable experience.

Invariably adv.

Meaning

不变地

Example

Their children invariably follow in their footsteps.

Irony n.

Meaning

反话

Example

His writing contains a cheerful touch of irony.

Irrigate v.

Meaning

灌溉

Example

The water in Lake Powell is used to irrigate the area.

Item n.

Meaning

条款

Example

The other item on the agenda is the tour.

Jaw n.

Meaning

颚

Jet lag n.

Meaning

时差反应

Example

Every time I fly to the States, I get really bad jet lag.

Junk n.

Meaning

无用的东西

Example

We ought to clear out this cupboard - it’s full of junk.

Jury n.

Meaning

陪审团

Example

The jury found him guilty of murdering three people.

Kin n.

Meaning

亲属

Example

We can’t release his name until we have informed his next of kin.

Knit n.

Meaning

编织

Example

She’s knitting a sweater.

Knot n.

Meaning

（绳的）结

Example

Let’s make a long rope by tying the two ropes together with a secure knot.

Lad n.

Meaning

男孩

List 20

Lap v.

Meaning

拍打

Example

The sea lapped at their ankles.

Lass n.

Meaning

女孩

Lavatory n.

Meaning

厕所

Layoff n.

Meaning

裁员

Example

Stock prices went down when the firm suddenly announced layoffs.

Layout n.

Meaning

布局

Example

Retailers can relieve the headaches by redesigning store layouts.

Lease v.

Meaning

出租

Example

The estate contains 300 new homes, about a third of which are leased to the council.

Legislation n.

Meaning

法律

Example

They build skills so that some day they might write a great novel, a piece of sorely needed legislation, or the perfect love letter.

Lens n.

Meaning

透镜

Example

I packed your sunglasses with the green lenses.

Lest conj.

Meaning

唯恐

Example

Helen turned the radio down lest she should miss the phone ringing.

Liable adj.

Meaning

可能的

Example

People found guilty of reckless driving may be liable to imprisonment or a fine.

Lick v.

Meaning

舔

Example

“It’s so sweet!” Mary says, licking the honey on the spoon.

Lily n.

Meaning

百合花

Limb n.

Meaning

肢

Example

The accident victims mostly had injuries to their lower limbs.

Liver n.

Meaning

肝

Example

Symptoms of the disease include an enlarged spleen or liver.

Lobby n.

Meaning

大厅

Loophole n.

Meaning

漏洞

Example

He exploited a legal loophole in order to avoid paying child support.

Lord n.

Meaning

领主

Example

She married a lord and lives in this huge house in the Cotswolds.

Lump v.

Meaning

将······归并在一起

Example

We can lump manufacturing and services together.
