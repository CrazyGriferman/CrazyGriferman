《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Nine

发表于 2019-09-03 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Business Meeting Words

Ascertain v.

Meaning

to determine with certainty

Derived from

a ➡️ to

certain

Example

We must ascertain that these figures are correct.

Augment v.

Meaning

to enlarge; to increase

Example

To succeed, we must augment our profits.

Buttress v.

Meaning

to strengthen or support

Example

You must buttress your argument with data.

Coalesce v.

Meaning

to unite by growth into one unit

Derived from

com- ➡️ together

alescere ➡️ to grow up

Example

These departments will coalesce into one unit.

Job Application Words

Commensurate adj.

Meaning

of equal proportion to something else

Derived from

com- ➡️ with

mensurare ➡️ to measure

Example

Same named a salary that would be commensurate with his ability.

Competence n.

Meaning

ability; the state of being able to perform a task

Example

Jane’s resume validated her competence.

Complaisant adj.

Meaning

agreeable; eager to please

Example

Ray made it obvious that he would be a complaisant employee.

Expertise n.

Meaning

specialized knowledge

Example

Mary’s education and experience gave her the expertise that the position required.

Indicative adj.

Meaning

pointing out; showing

Example

The reference letters were indicative of the young man’s ability to do a good job.

Interactive adj.

Meaning

capable of communicating and working with others

Example

Walter was hired because he was an interactive person who understood teamwork.

Business Troubles Vocabulary

Beleaguered v.

Meaning

to be faced with obstacles; to be overwhelmed by problems

Example

Facing bankruptcy, the beleaguered CEO suffered a heart attack.

Collusion n.

Meaning

an illegal conspiracy; a secret plot to do harm

Derived from

com- ➡️ together

ludere ➡️ to play

Example

The chairman of the board was accused of collusion with the vice president of the company in an attempt to replace its president.

Gamut n.

Meaning

a range of something

Example

Unable to save his failing business, Wilson experienced a gamut of emotions from anger to despair.

Onus n.

Meaning

a burden

Derived from

onus (Latin) ➡️ load, burden

Example

The onus of staggering debt was more than he could bear.

Opprobrium n.

Meaning

disgrace as a result of shameful behavior; reproach

Derived from

opprobrium (Latin) ➡️ disgrace, infamy

Example

Her rudeness in dealing with a client brought opprobrium from her supervisor.

Pep Talk Words

Glean v.

Meaning

to gather knowledge or information bit by bit

Example

A successful businessman will listen to and observe his employees in order to glean a better understanding of their personal and professional needs.

Gumption n.

Meaning

shrewd common sense

Example

The manager advised his team to use gumption when dealing with a difficult client.

Inculcate v.

Meaning

to teach by repetition; to impress an idea by urging or by example.

Derived from

in- ➡️ in

calcare ➡️ to tread, press in

Example

A good coach will inculcate her players to work and play as a team.

Laud v.

Meaning

to praise highly

Derived from

Mr.Harris was quick to laud a student’s improved performance.

Tutelage n.

Meaning

The act of giving close instruction

Derived from

Under the supervisor’s tutelage, the workers improved their work habits.

Daily Power Words

Cohort n.

Meaning

An associate or companion with shared interests

Derived from

com- ➡️ with

hortus ➡️ garden

Example

Meg and her cohort Sally opened their own veterinarian clinic.

Commiserate v.

Meaning

to share someone’s misfortune or sadness

Derived from

Example

Frank was the first to commiserate with Joe when the boy’s bike was stolen.

Innate adj.

Meaning

inborn; possessed as part of a person’s personality

Derived from

in- ➡️ in

nasci ➡️ to be born

Example

Her innate sense of integrity earned her a promotion.

Nebulous adj.

Meaning

vague, hazy

Derived from

nebulosus ➡️ cloudy, misty

Example

The speaker’s nebulous remarks were difficult to understand.

Nepotism n.

Meaning

favoritism toward a relative in a business situation

Example

Everyone resented their boss’s nepotism when he promoted a nephew in place of a better qualified employee.

Ostensibly adv.

Meaning

seeming or appearing to be true

Derived from

ob ➡️ in front of

tendere ➡️ to stretch

Example

He was ostensibly going on a business trip when in fact he went to visit a girlfriend.

Negotiation-related words

Assertive adj.

Meaning

inclined to self-confidence and boldness in expressing an opinion

Example

An experienced negotiator is assertive but calm and polite.

Belligerent adj.

Meaning

displaying angry, combative behavior

Derived from

bell ➡️ war

gerere ➡️ to bear, to carry

Example

A belligerent attitude indicates a weakness in a negotiator’s position.

Concession n.

Meaning

the act of yielding or giving in on some point in a negotiation

Example

A skilled negotiator knows when it is to her advantage to make a concession.

Consummate v.

Meaning

to finalize; to close a deal

Derived from

com- ➡️ together, with

summus ➡️ highest

Example

The goal of every negotiation is to reach an agreement and to consummate a settlement.

Reciprocity n.

Meaning

the act of exchanging favors; trading for a mutual benefit

Example

Reciprocity plays a major role in the process of negotiation.
