六级疑难词汇 list 1~3

发表于 2019-09-27 | 更新于 2019-10-02 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

List 1

Abbreviation n.

Meaning

缩略语

Example

NO. is the abbreviation for Number.

Abide v.

Meaning

遵守

Example

Competitors must abide by the judge’s decision.

Abort v.

Meaning

中止（计划）

Example

The mission had to be aborted because of a technical problem.

Abortion n.

Meaning

堕胎

Example

Abortion is restricted in some American states.

Abreast adv.

Meaning

并排

Example

The path was so narrow that only two persons could walk abreast.

Abrupt adj.

Meaning

突然的

Example

The van came to an abrupt stop.

Absurd adj.

Meaning

荒谬的

Example

She makes the most absurd claims about her past achievement.

Abundance n.

Meaning

大量

Example

Now he finds immigrant craftsmen gathered in abundance outside his store in the early morning, waiting for it to open so they can buy supplies for the day’s work as contractors.

Accessory n.

Meaning

附件

Example

My girlfriend likes to buy some accessories.

Acclaim v.

Meaning

公开称赞

Example

Prejudice was acclaimed by the critics as a masterpiece.

Accord n.

Meaning

一致

Example

These results are in accord with earlier research.

Acid adj.

Meaning

尖刻的

Example

I was expecting his acid remarks, but he remained silent.

Acupuncture n.

Meaning

针灸

Example

Acupuncture originated in China.

Adhere v.

Meaning

粘附

Example

It’s the sort of surface that paint doesn’t adhere to very well.

Adjacent adj.

Meaning

邻近的

Example

The site of the new building is adjacent to the park.

Adjoin v.

Meaning

贴近

Example

The stables adjoin the west wing of the house.

Adore v.

Meaning

爱慕

Example

She absolutely adores her nieces and nephews.

Advent n.

Meaning

到来

Example

Since the advent of computer, there have been great changes in our life.

Adverse adj.

Meaning

不利的

Example

Climate change is likely to have adverse impacts on human health.

Aesthetic adj.

Meaning

美学的

Example

They often give their readers aesthetic satisfaction.

Affiliate v.

Meaning

使附属于

Example

Mary is affiliated with no party.

Affirm v.

Meaning

断言

Example

The suspect affirmed that he had been at home all evening.

Affix v.

Meaning

粘贴

Example

You’d better affix your seal to the contract.

Afflict v.

Meaning

使折磨

Example

The same economic problems afflict Great Britain.

Afloat adj.

Meaning

（在水上）漂浮的

Example

We managed to get the boat afloat again.

Aftermath n.

Meaning

（战争、事故、坏事情等的）后果

Example

How would they cope with the aftermath of the war?

Aggregate n.

Meaning

总数

Example

In the aggregate, women outlive men by 7 or more years.

Agitate v.

Meaning

鼓动

Example

The women who worked in these mills had begun to agitate for better conditions.

Agony n.

Meaning

痛苦

Example

The criminal’s face was marked with guilt and agony.

Aide n.

Meaning

助手

Example

The president travels with a large retinue of aides and bodyguards.

Ail v.

Meaning

（使）患病

Example

Grandpa is ailing and has to stay in bed.

Airborne adj.

Meaning

空气传播的

Example

Infections can be passed around by airborne particles.

Akin adj.

Meaning

相似的

Example

They speak a language akin to French.

Ale n.

Meaning

麦芽酒

Align v.

Meaning

（使）结盟

Example

Many women do not want to align themselves with the movement.

List 2

Allergic adj.

Meaning

过敏的

Example

I’d like to exchange the shirt. I’ve learned that the person I bought it for is allergic to wool.

Alliance n.

Meaning

结盟

Example

NATO is a formal military alliance with America as its head.

Allot v.

Meaning

分配

Example

The boss allotted us two weeks to complete the task.

Alloy n.

Meaning

合金

Example

It was the first alloy William forged.

Allude v.

Meaning

暗指

Example

Lucy often alludes that she wants a wealthy marriage.

Allure v.

Meaning

诱惑

Example

High profits allured many investors to put money in it.

Almighty adj.

Meaning

全能的

Aloft adv.

Meaning

在空中

Example

We held our glasses aloft.

Amass v.

Meaning

积聚

Example

They amassed enough evidence to convict her.

Amend v.

Meaning

修改

Example

We’ll have to amend our reports.

Amiable adj.

Meaning

友好的

Example

It is said that the new manager is amiable, different from the last one.

Ammunition n.

Meaning

弹药

Example

The machine gun ran out of ammunition.

Amplify v.

Meaning

增强

Example

They used mirrors to amplify the amount of light in a room.

Analogue adj.

Meaning

模拟的

Example

The new analogue device is very complicated.

Analogy n.

Meaning

类比

Example

He uses the analogy of the family to explain the role of the state.

Anecdote n.

Meaning

轶事

Example

This book is a collection of anecdotes of famous people.

Anguish v.

Meaning

使极度痛苦

Example

The anguish of knowing that we could do nothing to help was almost unbearable.

Animate v.

Meaning

使具有活力

Example

Jim is a famous actor in comedy and his arrival to grandmother’s birthday animates the whole party.

Annex v.

Meaning

并吞

Example

Britain annexed this small island west of Scotland in 1955.

Anonymous adj.

Meaning

匿名的

Example

The bomb threat was made by an anonymous caller.

Antagonism n.

Meaning

对抗情绪

Example

It was difficult to explain their antagonism towards strangers.

Antenna n.

Meaning

触角

Antibiotic n.

Meaning

抗生素

Example

Now we have lots of antibiotics that kill bacteria.

Antonym n.

Meaning

反义词

Example

Two antonyms of ‘light’ are ‘dark’ and ‘heavy’.

Appall v.

Meaning

使惊骇

Example

We were appalled by his behavior.

Apparatus n.

Meaning

器具

Example

Astronauts have special breathing apparatus.

Appease v.

Meaning

平息

Example

They attempted to appease international opposition by promising to hold talks.

Appendix n.

Meaning

阑尾

Example

The endangered species are listed under Appendix 1.

Appraisal n.

Meaning

评价

Example

An investment appraisal comes with the project.

Apprentice n.

Meaning

学徒

Example

Mom intends to make me apprentice to the old blacksmith.

Appropriate v.

Meaning

私吞

Example

Tom is suspected of appropriating government funds.

Apron n.

Meaning

停机坪

Example

You’re 25 years old, and you still haven’t cut the apron strings.

Apt adj.

Meaning

恰当的

Example

Love at first sight is a very apt description of how Jimmy felt when he saw Flora.

Arbitrate v.

Meaning

做出公断

Example

The couple asked the counselor to arbitrate their argument.

Arc n.

Meaning

弧

Example

The mountains formed an arc around the north of the city.

Archive v.

Meaning

把······存档

Example

This company has archived its printed advertisements.

Ardent adj.

Meaning

热烈的

Example

Did you feel self-satisfied with the result of your ardent labors?

Arena n.

Meaning

竞技场

Example

Today, businesses must be able to compete in the international arena.

Aristocrat n.

Meaning

贵族

Example

Many aristocrats were killed in the French Revolution.

Armor n.

Meaning

盔甲

Example

In the past, knights used to wear suits of armor.

Array n.

Meaning

一系列

Example

An array of solar panels supplies the site with electricity.

Arrogant adj.

Meaning

傲慢的

Example

I found him arrogant and rude.

Artery n.

Meaning

干线

Example

Clarence Street was one of the north-bound arteries of the central business district.

Articulate v.

Meaning

明确有力地表达

Example

I am unable to articulate the sensation.

Artifact n.

Meaning

人工制品

Example

The big bell is an artifact that is heavy.

Artillery n.

Meaning

大炮

Example

Naval gunfire and ground-based artillery are generally less accurate than many aircraft-borne weapons.

Ascend v.

Meaning

攀登

Example

They began slowly ascending the rock face.

Ascertain v.

Meaning

弄清

Example

Police are trying to ascertain the facts of the case.

Ascribe v.

Meaning

把······归因于

Example

An autopsy eventually ascribed the baby’s death to sudden infant death syndrome.

Meaning

Example

At that time, all business men aspired to go to China.

Assassinate v.

Meaning

暗杀

Example

The question was who had hired them to assassinate Rosenberg.

Assault n.

Meaning

攻击

Example

It appeared that the assault had never occurred.

Assimilate v.

Meaning

使同化

Example

They have assimilated other cultures and religions.

Assorted adj.

Meaning

各种各样的

Example

It should be a great week, with overnight stops in assorted hotels in the West Highlands.

Astray adv.

Meaning

迷路地

Example

If it hadn’t been for him, we would have gone astray.

Asylum n.

Meaning

庇护

Example

More than half a million people sought asylum in Europe last year.

Atlas n.

Meaning

地图集

Example

For those of you who don’t have an atlas to hand, Newcastle is a city in the north-east of England.

Atrocity n.

Meaning

残暴行为

Example

Some of the worst atrocities in the Yugoslav war took place at a farm near Sarajevo.

Attendant n.

Meaning

侍者

Example

We’ll have an attendant with him 24 hours a day to keep an eye on him.

Auction n.

Meaning

拍卖

Example

They bought the paintings at auction in 1989.

Audit v.

Meaning

审计

Example

The account has not been audited yet.

Augment v.

Meaning

Example

The book will tell you how to augment your wealth.

Authoritative adj.

Meaning

有权威性的

Example

Edward seems very authoritative in his report.

Autonomous adj.

Meaning

自治的

Example

They proudly declared themselves part of a new autonomous province.

Auxiliary adj.

Meaning

Esperanto is an artificial language, designed to serve internationally as an auxiliary means of communication among speakers of different languages.

Avail n.

Meaning

帮助

Example

My attempts to improve the situation were of little avail.

Avenge v.

Meaning

报仇

Example

The man took an oath to avenge his father’s murder.

Avert v.

Meaning

转移

Example

Kevin could not avert his eyes from that beautiful girl.

Axis n.

Meaning

中心线

Example

The door to the room pivots on an axis.

Barge n.

Meaning

驳船

Example

Carrying goods by train costs nearly three times more than carrying them by barge.

Barometer n.

Meaning

气压机

Example

The barometer‘s fall means it is going to rain.

Barracks n.

Meaning

兵营

Example

The beggars were lodged in the old army barracks.

Barren adj.

Meaning

贫瘠的

Example

We drove through a barren rocky landscape.

Barricade n.

Meaning

栅栏

Example

The fans were kept back behind barricades.

Bass n.

Meaning

低音部

Example

He sings bass in the church choir.

Batter v.

Meaning

连续猛击

Example

Adam was battered on the head with a cricket bat.

Bead n.

Meaning

珠子

Example

The hostess bought a bead curtain for her new house.

Beak n.

Meaning

鸟喙

Example

When the dove holds the branch of the tree in its beak, it is as if the tree and the dove join together to become one.

Beetle n.

Meaning

Example

I like Volkswagen Beetle Karmann so much.

Belly n.

Meaning

Example

Our first priority is putting food in empty bellies.

Meaning

Example

The patient was comforted by her doctor that the tumor was benign.

Beset v.

Meaning

困扰

Example

We were beset by mosquitoes in the forest.
