Opera Vocabulary

Aria n.

Meaning

a song for a single voice, designed to express the inner emotions of the character, either sad or joyous.

Example

The beautiful, heart-rending aria brought tears to Carol’s eyes.

Basso profundo n.

Meaning

a deep bass singing voice

Example

Paul Robeson and Feodor Chalapin were famous for their basso profundos.

Coloratura n.

Meaning

A soprano singer who specializes in high, light, flexible passages

Example

The coloratura‘s clean, high notes, trills, and runs thrilled her audience.

Libretto n.

Meaning

a book containing the words of an opera

Derived from

libro ➡️ book

Example

The libretto for the opera Faust was based on a medieval legend.

Overture n.

Meaning

an orchestral prelude, or introduction, to an opera that sets the tone and mood for the dramatic performances.

Example

The somber overture sent shivers down Dora’s spine.

Music Vocabulary

Concerto n.

Meaning

a composition for a solo instrument in three movements, accompanied by a symphonic orchestra.

Example

Mendelssohn’s Violin Concerto in E Minor was Allison’s favorite composition.

Crescendo n.

Meaning

a gradual increase in volume

Example

The conductor of the symphony orchestra signaled the string section to begin a crescendo.

Diminuendo n.

Meaning

a gradual decrease in volume

Example

The pianist lightened his touch as he began a diminuendo leading to the end of the piece.

Fortissimo adj.

Meaning

very loud

Example

The symphony ended with a grand fortissimo.

Ballet Vocabulary

Arabesque n.

Meaning

a ballet position in which you lift one leg up behind you

Example

Standing on one toe, the ballerina executed a perfect arabesque.

Battement n.

Meaning

a leap executed while beating the legs against each other

Example

The audience applauded Michael’s rapid battement.

Choreographer n.

Meaning

the creator of moves and figures for a ballet

Derived from

khoreia (Greek) ➡️ dance

graphein ➡️ to write

Example

The success of a ballet depends on the imagination and skill of the choreographer.

Cubism n.

Meaning

a school of modern art concerned with abstract and geometric representations rather than a realistic interpretation of nature

Example

Cubism is an interesting art form, although it may be difficult to understand.

Impressionism n.

Meaning

a new technique and approach to painting originating in France in the later part of the nineteenth century.

Example

Renoir, Manet, and Monet were among the leaders of impressionism. Their paintings are still greatly admired and valued.

Renaissance n.

Meaning

The transition period between the Middle Ages and modern times characterized by a revival of artistic and intellectual achievement.

Color Vocabulary

Aureolin n.

Meaning

a bright, yellow pigment made from cobalt and potassium

Example

Aureolin is used in watercolors and oil paints.

Cobalt blue adj.

Meaning

A deep blue to greenish-blue pigment made of oxide of cobalt and aluminum.

Example

The artist’s model wore a cobalt blue gown.

Magenta adj.

Meaning

a strong purple-red pigment

Example

The chemical discovery of magenta occurred about a hundred and fifty years ago.

Nigrescence n.

Meaning

the process of becoming black or dark

Example

The painting emphasized the nigrescence of the thundercloud.

Vermilion n.

Meaning

a durable bright red pigment

Example

Vermilion is obtained by grinding the mineral cinnabar into a fine powder.

Poetic Vocabulary

Alliteration n.

Meaning

a series of two or more words with the same initial letter or sound.

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

littera ➡️ letter, script

Example

The phrase “only a lone loon crying” uses the I sound as alliteration.

Iambic n.

Meaning

a poem in which the second word or syllable in a line of poetry is stressed

Example

“My country, ‘tis of thee” is written with an iambic rhythm

Sonnet n.

Meaning

a fourteen-line poem that has various rhyming schemes and follows a specific format

Example

Shakespeare wrote many sonnets that are now well-known.

Stanza n.

Meaning

a division of a poem that contains a certain number of lines of verse grouped in a definite scheme

Example

This poem had sixteen lines and was divided into four stanzas with four verse lines in each stanza.

Trochaic adj.

Meaning

A rhythm that stresses the first syllable or word in a line of poetry

Example

“Yankee Doodle went to town” illustrates the trochaic rhythm. The song begins with the accent on the first syllable of the word Yankee.
