List 10

Discord n.

Meaning

不一致

Example

There is discord on the matter during the meeting.

Discourse n.

Meaning

演讲

Example

Gates responds with a lengthy discourse on deployment strategy.

Discreet adj.

Meaning

谨慎的

Example

The drivers should drive at a discreet distance especially on rainy days.

Discrepancy n.

Meaning

不符合之处

Example

I have an unexplained discrepancy with you.

Discrete adj.

Meaning

分离的

Example

The change happens in a series of discrete steps.

Disgust v.

Meaning

厌恶

Example

Many parents are disgusted by the amount of violence in the film.

Dismantle v.

Meaning

废除

Example

A combination of public policy decisions and corporate scheming saw to it that countless convenient and efficient urban streetcar and intra-city rail systems were dismantled.

Dismay v.

Meaning

使失望

Example

I was dismayed at the ignorance and poverty around me.

Dispatch v.

Meaning

派遣

Example

Shall I dispatch him for you?

Dispense v.

Meaning

分发

Example

Villagers dispensed tea to visitors.

Disperse v.

Meaning

散去

Example

The crowd dispersed peacefully.

Displace v.

Meaning

取代

Example

Many feared that e-book would displace the paper book.

Disposal n.

Meaning

由······某人自行支配

Example

Students have a well-stocked library at their disposal.

Dispose v.

Meaning

解决

Example

The idea at least disposes of the urgent problem.

Disposition n.

Meaning

性格

Example

Janet has a sociable and childlike disposition.

Disregard v.

Meaning

漠视

Example

The man disregarded Tommy’s question and stared at his daughter with sincere concern.

Dissent v.

Meaning

持异议

Example

Everyone has the right to dissent.

Dissertation n.

Meaning

专题论文

Example

What I’d like to do in this session is to give you the opportunity to ask questions on writing the dissertation.

Dissident n.

Meaning

持不同政见者

Example

Tom was exiled as a dissident several years ago and now he would come back.

Dissipate v.

Meaning

消散

Example

The sun began to rise; the fog dissipated slowly.

Distill v.

Meaning

吸取

Example

My father gave me his useful advice distilled from his lifetime’s experience.

Distinct adj.

Meaning

与其他不同的

Example

They were classified into two distinct groups.

Distort v.

Meaning

扭曲

Example

Bob’s face was distorted in pains.

Disturbance n.

Meaning

混乱

Example

There were disturbances in the crowd as fans left the stadium.

Divert v.

Meaning

转移（注意力）

Example

I tried in vain to divert his mind from the matter.

Doctrine n.

Meaning

教条

Example

They held similar doctrines about humanity.

Dodge v.

Meaning

回避

Example

Linda crossed the road, dodging the traffic skillfully.

Dole v.

Meaning

发放

Example

Vera was doling out candy to the kids.

Domain n.

Meaning

领域

Example

We will bring him here, into my domain.

Dome n.

Meaning

穹顶

Dough n.

Meaning

生面团

Example

As the bread dough rises, it will double in size.

Doze v.

Meaning

打瞌睡

Example

The office was so hot that I nearly dozed off at my desk.

Drainage n.

Meaning

排水系统

Example

The city was equipped with a drainage system.

Drastic adj.

Meaning

激烈的

Example

Drastic changes are needed if environmental catastrophe is to be avoided.

Dread n.

Meaning

担忧

Example

I have to go to the dentist’s tomorrow, and I’m dreading it.

Drill v.

Meaning

训练

Example

We had a chance to drill out English with foreigners.

Dub v.

Meaning

起······绰号

Example

It’s a British film dubbed into French.

Dubious adj.

Meaning

犹豫不决的

Example

The result of the negotiation is still dubious.

Dwarf v.

Meaning

使显得矮小

Example

Many children in Africa are dwarfed by insufficient food.

Dwell v.

Meaning

居住

Example

I found a new place to dwell.

Eccentric adj.

Meaning

怪癖的

Example

They are used to his eccentric ways.

Eclipse v.

Meaning

使暗淡

Example

The outstanding performance eclipsed the previous record.

Edible adj.

Meaning

可食用的

Example

The fridge contains almost nothing edible.

elastic adj.

Example

移居国外

Example

All formalities have to be done before you can emigrate.

Example

Empirical adj.

Meaning

经验的

Example

I find no empirical evidence to support this argument.

Emulate v.

Meaning

Example

Enact v.

Meaning

颁布

Example

Members of legislature are elected by the people to enact laws.

Enclosure n.

Meaning

附件

Example

There are several enclosures in the envelop.

Encyclopedia n.

Meaning

百科全书

Example

This encyclopedia is truly a compendium of knowledge.

Endeavor v.

Meaning

努力

Example

We always endeavor to please our customers.

Endorse v.

Meaning

支持

Example

For example, men who endorse traditional masculine norms report lower relationship satisfaction, as do their female partners.

Endow v.

Meaning

资助

Example

The state of Michigan has endowed three institutes to do research for industry.

Enforce v.

Meaning

强迫

Example

If the judgment is enforced, Alex will be bankrupted .

Enlist v.

Meaning

征募

Example

Politicians are beginning to realize the importance of enlisting psychologists’s help in fighting climate change.

Ensue v.

Meaning

发生

Example

After the heavy rain, floods ensued.

Entail v.

Meaning

需要

Example

What exactly does this job entail?

Entity n.

Meaning

实体

Example

The mind exists as a separate entity.

Entrust v.

Meaning

委托给

Example

All employees entrusted with the care of children are checked by the police

Envelop v.

Meaning

包住

Example

More dense atmosphere gradually enveloped Earth.

Envisage v.

Meaning

想象

Example

The changes have been greater than we ever envisaged.

Envoy n.

Meaning

使者

Example

The United Nations is sending a special envoy to the area.

Epic n.

Meaning

史诗

Example

Contemporary readers, forgetting the origins of western epic, lyric, and dramatic forms, are easily disposed to think of ‘literature’ only as something written.

Epoch n.

Meaning

时代

Example

His theory marks a new epoch in physics.

Equate v.

Meaning

（使）等同

Example

Most people equate wealth with success.

Equator n.

Meaning

赤道

Example

Singapore lies on the Equator.

Equity n.

Meaning

公平

Example

Look at any global survey of gender equity and Sweden will be near the top.

Eradicate v.

Meaning

根除

Example

The debt problems of some enterprises have been mostly eradicated over the years.

Erode v.

Meaning

侵蚀

Example

This system of production eroded workers’ condition of labor.

Erosion n.

Meaning

侵蚀

Example

Although the increase in rainfall would aid irrigation, it would add to the problems of soil erosion.

Erroneous adj.

Meaning

错误的

Example

The advocators of this erroneous theory are falling away.

Escalate v.

Meaning

（使）逐步扩大

Example

They said they didn’t want to escalate the war.

Escort v.

Meaning

护送

Example

The shipment was escorted by guards.

Example

Meanwhile, New Orleans officials have crafted a plan to use buses and trains to evacuate the sick and the disabled.

Evade v.

Example

I could tell that Tim was trying to evade the issue.

Example

We just make some half-hearted resolution and it evaporates after a week or two.

Exceptional adj.

Example

A few of the top executives are women, but this is still exceptional.

Meaning

Example

Example

Example

Expel v.

Example

Exponent n.

Example

Exposition n.

Example

Exquisite adj.

Meaning

Example

Steven corrected himself with an exquisite sense of grammar.

Extract v.

Example

He opened a drawer and extracted a file.

Extravagant adj.

Meaning

Example

John was so extravagant that he was always in debt.

Example

Facet n.

Example

Example

The counsellor may be able to facilitate communication between the couple.

Feast n.
