#Bene-
词根意思:

well

单词:

1.benediction

2.benefactor

3.beneficiary

4.benevolence

##Benediction
意思:

a prayer that asks for God’s blessing, especially a prayer that includes a worship service.

一开始看到注释的时候以为prayer是”祭司”的意思，之后一查是”祷告”的意思。一看到er就以为是职业了。
注意 especially后面的内容

记忆：

bene- ➡️ well

dictio- ➡️ speaking

运用语境:

Perhaps the best-known benediction is the so-called Aaronic Benediction from the Bible, which begins,”May the Lord bless you and keep you.”

分析可知benediction主要运用于宗教场合，特别是描述有关基督教的宗教活动。

例句：

1.The moment the bishop had finished his benediction, she squeezed quickly out of her row and darted out the cathedral’s side entrance.

2.Many Christians go to church every week to listen to ministers’ benediction.

#Benefactor
意思:

Someone who helps another person or group, especially by giving money.

特别针对给钱的

记忆:

bene- ➡️ well
facere ➡️ to do

运用语境:

A benefactor may be involved in almost any field.

分析可知benefactor可用于任意领域，只要是与捐赠钱相关的

例句：

1.An anonymous benefactor had given $15 million to establish an ecological institute at the university.

2.Bill Gates is one of the famous benefactors in the world who once donated a building to Carnegie Mellon University.

#Beneficiary

意思:

A person or organization that benefits or is expected to benefit from something, especially one that receives money or property when someone dies.

主要和人寿保险，或者继承遗产有关？

记忆:

bene- ➡️ well

ficus ➡️ making,doing

运用语境:

Beneficiary is often used in connection with life insurance, but it shows up in many other contexts as well.

捐赠、商业和许多其它领域都可使用，但最普遍的是用于保险

例句:

1.Living in a trailer in near-poverty, she received word in the mail that her father had died, naming her as the sole beneficiary of his life-insurance policy.

2.Since John is the only child in his family, he must be the sole beneficiary of his parent’s life-insurance.

#Benevolence

意思:

Kindness, generosity

应该是这两个单词的统一替换？

记忆：

bene- ➡️ well

velle ➡️ to wish

运用语境：

Benevolence suggests an expansive and good-humoured tolerance and sympathy for others.

来源: Use the right word

To be benevolent, it helps to have money, but it’s not necessary, kind assistance of a non-financial sort may turn out to be lifesaving benevolence as well.

分析可知benevolence的运用场景较广，不只是物质上的帮助，精神上的帮助也可算作benevolence.

例句

1.In those financially desperate years, the young couple was saved only by the benevolence of her elderly great-uncle.

2.Thanks to the benevolence of his cousin, he is able to afford to buy this car.

#Am-
词根意思:

to love

单词:

1.amicable

2.enamored

3.amorous

4.paramour

##Amicable

意思:

Friendly, peaceful

似乎是这两个单词的同意替代

记忆:

am- ➡️ to love

amicabilis ➡️ friendly

运用语境:

Amicable often describes relations between two groups, or especially two nations.

When amicable describes more personal relations, it may indicate a rather formal friendliness.

分析可知改词主要运用于两个组织尤其是国家之间，且用于较为正式的场合。(也可用于人与人之间)

例句:

1.Their relations with their in-laws were generally amicable, despite some bickering during the holidays.

2.China usually helps Pakistan both financially and physically so that the relationship between China and Pakistan is amicable.

##Enamored

意思:

Charmed or fascinated; inflamed with love.

记忆:

am- ➡️ to love

en- ➡️ in、into

运用语境:

we also often use the word in negative contexts.
Note that both of and with are commonly used after enamored.

该词多用于贬义语境，但也可以用在褒义语境

例句:

1.Rebecca quickly became enamored of the town’s rustic surroundings, its slow pace, and its eccentric characters.

感觉这里的 enamored of 可以替换为 fascinated by？

2.Because of Minecraft, i become enamored of JAVA.

##Amorous

意思:

Having or showing strong feelings of attraction or love.

这里的attraction情感上的更少，肉体上的更多。

记忆:

amor ➡️ love, affection

运用语境:

the word is often used a bit sarcastically

该词多用于讽刺场景，多为贬义

例句:

1.It turned out that the amorous Congressman had gotten his girlfriend a good job and was paying for her apartment.

2.Since the custom of Chinese culture is conservative, we are not encouraged to be amorous.

##Paramour

意思:

A lover, often secret, not allowed by law or custom.

记忆:

par amour ➡️ through love (From French)

运用语境:

Since par amour meant “through love,” it implies a relationship based solely on love, often physical love, rather than on social custom or ceremony.

Today it tends to refer to the lover of a married man or woman, but may be used for any lover who isn’t obeying the social rules.

贬义，用于违背社会伦理的人身上。

例句:

1.He had been coming to the house for two years before her brothers realized that he was actually the paramour of their shy and withdrawn sister.

2.We couldn’t find paramours if we have already married

#Bell-
词根意思:

war

单词：

1.antebellum

2.bellicose

3.belligerence

4.rebellion

##Antebellum

意思:

Existing before a war, especially before the American Civil War (1861–65).

记忆:

ante ➡️ before

bell ➡️ war

运用语境:

Antebellum often summons up images of ease, elegance, and entertainment that disappeared in the postwar years.

主要运用于描述战前的美好场景。

例句:

1.When World War I was over, the French nobility found it impossible to return to their extravagant antebellum way of life.

2.After a war, refugees often want to return to the antebellum way of life.

##Bellicose

意思:

Warlike, aggressive, quarrelsome.

记忆:

bell- ➡️ war

运用语境:

Since bellicose describes an attitude that hopes for actual war, the word is generally applied to nations and their leaders.

主要运用在一个国家或者其领导人上

例句:

1.The more bellicose party always got elected whenever there was tension along the border and the public believed that military action would lead to security.

2.Since we have been constantly calling for a peaceful world, bellicose parties may not be welcomed by citizens.

##Belligerence

意思:

Aggressiveness

记忆:

bell- ➡️ war

运用语境:

Unlike bellicose and bellicosity, the word belligerence can be used at every level from the personal to the global.

不仅可以运用于个人，也可运用于集体

例句:

1.The belligerence in guard’s voice told them that the warning was a serious threat.

2.the belligerence in Tom’s voice shows he really wants to fight against the thieves

##Rebellion

意思:

Open defiance and opposition, sometimes armed, to a person or thing in authority.

表示反抗

记忆：

re- ➡️ back, again

bell- ➡️ war

运用语境:

a rebellion usually involves a group.

Rebellion, armed or otherwise, has often alerted those in power that those they control are very unhappy.

发起者应该是一个组织

例句:

1.A student rebellion that afternoon in Room 13 resulted in the new substitute teacher racing out of the building in tears.

2.ISIS launched a series of rebellion against local authority

#Pac-

词根意思:

agree or peace

单词:

1.pacify

2.pacifist

3.pact

4.pace

##Pacify

意思:

(1) To soothe anger or anxiety.

soothe:

to make someone more calm and more relaxed when they are feeling nervous, worried, or upset

(2) To subdue by armed action.

subdue:

to hold someone and make them stop behaving in an uncontrolled or violent way

记忆:

Pac- ➡️ peace

运用语境:

Someone stirred up by a strong emotion can usually be pacified by some kind words and the removal of its causes.

一般都是平息愤怒、焦虑

或者是制服或镇压暴动等等

例句:

1.It took the police hours to pacify the angry demonstrators.

2.A best friend is the person who can pacify your anger or anxiety under extreme circumstances.

##Pacifist

意思:

A person opposed to war or violence, especially someone who refuses to bear arms or to fight, on moral or religious grounds.

注意更多地是指那些基于道德或者是宗教原因而反对战争的人

记忆:

pac- ➡️ peace

运用语境:

Pacifists haven’t always met with sympathy or understanding

Refusing to fight ever, for any reason, calls for strong faith in one’s own moral or religious convictions.

运用于描述那些出于宗教和道德而反战的人

例句:

1.Her grandfather had fought in the Marines in World War II, but in his later years he had become almost a pacifist, opposing every war for one reason or another.

2.Many people believing in buddhism are pacifists.

##Pact

意思:

An agreement between two or more people or groups; a treaty or formal agreement between nations to deal with a problem or to resolve a dispute.

记忆:

pac- ➡️ agree or peace

运用语境:

Pact has “peace” at its root because a pact often ends a period of unfriendly relations.

The word is generally used in the field of international relations, where diplomats may speak of an “arms pact,” a “trade pact,” or a “fishing-rights pact.”

But it may also be used for any solemn agreement or promise between two people.

该词常用于国际关系领域，但也可以用于两个人之间的协议或承诺。

例句:
1.The girls made a pact never to reveal what had happened on that terrifying night in the abandoned house.

2.Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact was made to divide military areas between CCCP and German in Eastern Europe.

##Pace

意思:

Contrary to the opinion of

记忆:

pac- ➡️ agree

运用语境:

Writers use it when correcting an opinion that many people believe

用于修正人们错误的共识的场合

例句:

1.She had only three husbands, pace some Hollywood historians who claim she had as many as six.

2.Playing games properly is beneficial to us both physically and mentally, pace the misunderstanding that playing games is harmful to our health.

#Crim-
词根意思：

“fault or crime” or “accusation”

单词:

1.criminology

2.decriminalize

3.incriminate

4.recrimination

##Criminology

意思：

The study of crime, criminals, law enforcement, and punishment.

记忆：

crim- ➡️ fault or crime

logos ➡️ science

运用语境：

Criminology includes the study of all aspects of crime and law enforcement—criminal psychology, the social setting of crime, prohibition and prevention, investigation and detection, capture and punishment.

该单词主要运用于描述有关犯罪的学科

例句：

1.His growing interest in criminology led him to become a probation officer.

##Decriminalize

意思：

To remove or reduce the criminal status of.

记忆：

de- ➡️ away from

crim- ➡️ crime

运用语境：

Decriminalization is not the same as legalization; decriminalization may still call for a small fine (like a traffic ticket), and may apply only to use or possession of something, leaving the actual sale of goods or services illegal.

应该是和犯罪相关，我想应该是减刑的意思？

造句：

1.An angry debate over decriminalizing doctor-assisted suicide raged all day in the statehouse.

##Incriminate

意思：

To show evidence of involvement in a crime or a fault.

记忆：

in- ➡️ not

crim- ➡️ crime

运用语境：

The word doesn’t always refer to an actual crime. We can say, for instance, that a virus has been incriminated as the cause of a type of cancer, or that video games have been incriminated in the decline in study skills among young people.

我想这个单词应该是有指证的意思，在犯罪领域之外，它还可以被用于各种领域，我想应该可以可以理解为“是···的诱因”？

造句：

1.The muddy tracks leading to and from the cookie jar were enough to incriminate them.

##Recrimination

意思：

(1) An accusation in answer to an accusation made against oneself.

(2) The making of such an accusation.

记忆：

re- ➡️ back;again

crim- ➡️ accusation

运用语境：

An actual crime isn’t generally involved, but it may be.

Divorces and child-custody battles usually involve recriminations between husband and wife.

根据这个re的词根，我想这个词应该有“再控告”的意思，而且不一定运用于有人犯罪的场面，也许是为了其他的纠葛，这里词根的意思应该是指“accusation”。

造句：

1.Their failure to find help led to endless and pointless recriminations over responsibility for the accident.

#Prob-

词根意思：

“prove or proof” or “honesty and integrity”

单词：

1.approbation

2.probate

3.probity

4.reprobate

##Approbation

意思：

1.A formal or official act of approving

2.praise, usually given with pleasure or enthusiasm.

记忆：

prob- ➡️ prove

运用语境：

Approbation a noun form of approve, but approbation is usually stronger than mere approval.

An official commendation for bravery is an example of approbation

这种较为官方的，来自政府的也可以是approbation。

有个短语叫 social approbation ，这里的 approbation 可以翻译为 praise。

造句：

1.The senate signaled its approbation of the new plan by voting for it unanimously.

##Probate

意思：

1.The process of proving in court that the will of someone who has died is valid, and of administering the estate of a dead person.

记忆：

prob- ➡️ prove

运用语境：

Today we use probate more broadly to mean everything that’s handled in probate court, a special court that oversees the handling of estates (the money and property left when someone dies), making sure that everyone eventually receives what is properly theirs.

改词和继承遗产有关系，will可以被翻译为 “遗产”。如今这个单词可以用得更为广泛，指所有在继承遗产法庭上的东西。

造句：

1.When her father died, she thought she would be able to avoid probate, but she wasn’t that lucky.

##Probity

意思：

Absolute honesty and uprightness.

记忆:

prob- ➡️ honesty and integrity

运用语境：

Bankers, for example, have traditionally been careful to project an air of probity, even though banking scandals and bailouts have made this harder than ever.

其中“ project an air of probity ”这个短语很有意思

project 的意思应该是：estimate sth. on the basis of present trends

我觉得这个短语应该是说谎或者虚报夸大某事的意思。

Probity is a quality the public generally hopes for in its elected officials but doesn’t always get.

probity 应该是一种褒义的品质。

造句：

1.Her unquestioned probity helped win her the respect of her fellow judges.

##Reprobate

意思：

A person of thoroughly bad character.

记忆：

re- ➡️ against

prob- ➡️ honesty and intergrity

运用语境：

for many years reprobate has been said in a tone of joshing affection, usually to describe someone of doubtful morals but good humor.

也许如今这个词的用法更加褒义一点？形容那些道德不良但是极富幽默感的人。但我觉得还是有贬义成分的。

造句：

1.His wife finally left him, claiming he was a reprobate who would disappear for weeks at a time, gambling and drinking away all his money.

#Grav-

释义:

heavy, weighty, serious

单词：

1.grave

2.gravitas

3.gravitate

4.aggravate

##Grave

gravity n.

意思：

(1) Requiring serious thought or concern.

(2) Serious and formal in appearance or manner.

记忆：

grav- ➡️ serious

运用语境:

Gravity has a familiar physical meaning but also a nonphysical meaning—basically “seriousness.” Thus, something grave possesses gravity. You can refer to the gravity of a person’s manner

Or you can talk about a grave situation, as in the example sentence.

可以用于形容一个场合，也可以形容一个人的礼貌（？）

造句：

1.We realized that the situation was grave and that the slightest incident could spark all-out war.

##Gravitas

意思:

Great or very dignified seriousness.

记忆:

grav- ➡️ serious

运用语境：

We use gravitas today to identify the same solemn dignity in men and women, but it seems to come easier in those who are over 60, slow-moving—and a bit overweight.

比serious程度更深，常用于描述年龄较大，“身经百战”的的伯伯阿婆

造句：

1.The head of the committee never failed to carry herself with the gravitas she felt was appropriate to her office.

##Gravitate
意思：

To move or be drawn toward something, especially by natural tendency or as if by an invisible force.

记忆：

grav- ➡️ heavy, weighty

运用语境：

To gravitate is to respond, almost unconsciously, to a force that works like gravity to draw things steadily to it as if by their own weight.

Thus, young people gravitate toward a role model, moths gravitate to a flame, a conversation might gravitate toward politics, and everyone at a party often gravitates to the bar.

gravitate可以用于各种场景，主要是短语 “ gravitate toward ”，指无意识地吸引。

造句：

1.On hot evenings, the town’s social life gravitated toward the lakefront, where you could stroll the long piers eating ice cream or dance at the old Casino.

##Aggravate
意思：

(1) To make (an injury, problem, etc.) more serious or severe.

(2) To annoy or bother.

记忆：

grav- ➡️ serious

运用语境:

Since the grav- root means basically “weighty or serious,” the original meaning of aggravate was “to make more serious.”

A bad relationship with your parents can be aggravated by marrying someone who nobody likes.

when most people use aggravate today, they employ its “annoy” sense, as in “What really aggravates my dad is having to listen to that TV all day long.”

如今用得较多的是“惹恼”这个意思，不过如果是释义一，aggravate不仅仅是指伤口的恶化，也能指
一些事情的恶化，用途较广。

造句：

1.She went back to the soccer team before the knee was completely healed, which naturally aggravated the injury.

#Lev-

释义：

to raise or lighten

单词：

1.alleviate

2.elevation

3.cantilever

4.levity

##Alleviate

意思：

To lighten, lessen, or relieve, especially physical or mental suffering.

记忆：

lev- ➡️ to lighten

运用语境:

Physical pain or emotional anguish, or a water shortage or traffic congestion, can all be alleviated by providing the appropriate remedy.

这个单词应该是“减轻”的意思，但主要应该用于伤口的疼痛，而且“减轻”并不等同于“治愈”，所以要和 cure 区分。

造句:

1.Cold compresses alleviated the pain of the physical injury, but only time could alleviate the effect of the insult.

##Elevation

意思：

(1) The height of a place.

(2) The act or result of lifting or raising someone or something.

记忆:

lev- ➡️ ️ to raise

运用语境：

Psychologists use the term “mood elevation” to mean improvement in a patient’s depression.

Elevation can also mean “promotion”; thus, a vice president may be elevated to president, or a captain may be elevated to admiral.

elevation不仅可以指海拔，也可以运用于其他领域，可以被译为“晋升”，或在心理学中指代病人抑郁的“改善”。

造句:

1.Her doctor is concerned about the elevation of her blood pressure since her last visit.

##Cantilever
意思：

A long piece of wood, metal, etc., that sticks out from a wall to support something above it.

记忆:

lev- ➡️ to raise

运用语境：

Cantilevers hold up a surface or room without themselves being supported at their outer end.

应该没有衍生意思，主要就是指“支柱”

造句:

1.The house’s deck, supported by cantilevers, jutted out dramatically over the rocky slope, and looking over the edge made him dizzy.

##Levity

意思：

Lack of appropriate seriousness.

记忆：

lev- ➡️ to lighten

运用语境：

Levity originally was thought to be a physical force exactly like gravity but pulling in the opposite direction, like the helium in a balloon.

Today levity refers only to lightness in manner. To stern believers of some religious faiths, levity is often regarded as almost sinful. But the word, like its synonym frivolity, now has an old-fashioned ring to it and is usually used only half-seriously.

levity最早应该是形容一种力，例如让气球上升的力，和词根lev有关，是向上的。

如今较多使用的是礼仪中人们的轻浮，使用的场景不是那么严肃，但也绝不是开玩笑用的。

造句:

1.The Puritan elders tried to ban levity of all sorts from the community’s meetings, but found it increasingly difficult to control the younger generation.
