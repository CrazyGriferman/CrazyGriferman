《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Five

发表于 2019-08-16 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

* Physical Location

Clavicle n.

Location

collarbone

Example

The clavicle connects the sternum with the shoulder blades.

Epiglottis n.

Location

throat

Derived from

epi- ➡️ on

glottis (Greek) ➡️ tongue

Example

The function of the epiglottis is to close your windpipe when you swallow food.

Esophagus n.

Location

throat
or
neck chest

Derived from

oisein (Greek) ➡️ to carry

phagein ➡️ to eat

Example

When you swallow, food goes down the esophagus to your stomach.

Eustachian tube n.

Location

ears

Example

The Eustachian tube leads from an opening in the middle ear to a cavity at the back of the nose. Children who have frequent colds often develop middle-ear infections (otitis media) via the Eustachian tubes.

Pectoral muscle n.

Location

chest

Example

Exercise can develop your pectoral muscles.

Pituitary gland n.

Location

left side of the head, under the brain

Example

The pituitary gland excretes vital hormones affecting growth and other aspects of physiological functions.

Sternum n.

Location

breastbone

Example

Upper ribs are attached to the sternum.

Symptoms

Asthma n.

Meaning

respiratory breathing problems
or
wheezing

Derived from

azein (Greek) ➡️ breathe hard

Example

An asthma attack requires immediate medical attention.

Dermatitis n.

Meaning

a skin rash that may be caused by an allergic reaction to something in the environment

Derived from

derma- ➡️ skin, hide

-itis ➡️ noun suffix

Example

A chemical in a cleanser that Sally used gave her a case of dermatitis.

* Gastroenteritis n.

Meaning

stomach and intestinal distress caused by a virus or food poisoning

Example

Tourists who travel to underdeveloped countries risk coming down with gastroenteritis.

* Polymyalgia rheumatica n.

Meaning

a fairly common disease involving muscles and joints

Example

Her polymyalgia rheumatica was treated with aspirin and steroids.

Vertigo n.

Meaning

dizziness

Derived from

vertere ➡️ to turn

Example

Mary’s vertigo was caused by a middle-ear infection that affected her sense of balance.

Medical terms that apply to practitioners

Aphasia n.

Meaning

a loss of speech

Derived from

a- ➡️ without

phasis ➡️ utterance

Example

A head injury caused her aphasia.

Audiologist n.

Meaning

a specialist trained in evaluating hearing loss

Derived from

audire (Latin) ➡️ to hear

-logy ➡️ brach of knowledge

Example

An audiologist prescribed a hearing aid.

Pathologist n.

Meaning

a scientist who studies and identifies diseases

Derived from

pathos (Greek) ➡️ suffering

-logia ➡️ study

Example

The pathologist studies a tissue sample with care.

Physiotherapist n.

Meaning

a specialist who deals with physical impairment

Derived from

physio- ➡️ physical

therapy

-ist ➡️ one who does or makes

Example

After his knee surgery, Jim was referred to a physiotherapist.

Masseuse n.

Meaning

a woman who uses massage to relieve muscular pain

Example

After several sessions with a masseuse, Amy began to feel better.

Diagnostic Test

Angiography n.

Meaning

a diagnostic procedure for examining the interior of blood vessels

Derived from

angio- ➡️ blood vessel

-graphy ➡️ process of writing or recording

Example

The angiography was a painless procedure.

Bronchoscopy n.

Meaning

a procedure for examining the bronchi and lungs by means of a flexible, lighted tube that is passed through the nose or mouth into the chest cavity (也称 支气管镜检查) (这英英我实在看不下去了:( )

Derived from

-scopy ➡️ viewing, examining

Example

Before being treated for a chronic cough, Jim underwent a bronchoscopy.

Cardiac catheterization n.

Meaning

an angiography of the heart, in which a tube is passed through a vein into the heart in order to investigate the heart at work

Example

The cardiac catheterization gave Ann a clean bill of health.

Echography n.

Meaning

a diagnostic procedure that uses ultrasound waves to detect possible abnormalities in the body

Derived from

echo

-graphy ➡️ process of writing or recording

Example

An echography revealed the presence of a tumor.

Endoscopy n.

Meaning

a procedure that examines the interior of the stomach by means of a flexible lighted tube

Derived from

endo- ➡️ inside, within

-scopy

-scopy ➡️ viewing, examining

Example

An endoscopy confirmed the doctor’s opinion that his patient had an ulcer.

Dental specialities and problems

Caries n.

Meaning

tooth decay, which can be treated by your family dentist

Derived from

caries (Latin) ➡️ rottenness, decay

Example

It is important to repair caries as soon as they appear.

Gingivitis n.

Meaning

A gun disease that can become a more serious condition called periodontitis

Derived from

gingiva (Latin) ➡️ gums

-itis ➡️ noun suffix

Example

Gingivitis should be treated by a periodontist.

Malocclusion n.

Meaning

a misalignment of teeth so that one is unable to bite or chew properly

Derived from

mal- ➡️ bad

occlusion ➡️ act of being stopped up

Example

Patients with malocclusion should see an orthodontist.
