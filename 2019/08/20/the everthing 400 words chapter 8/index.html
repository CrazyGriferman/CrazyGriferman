《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Eight

发表于 2019-08-20 | 更新于 2019-08-30 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Family-related Words

Abandonment n.

Meaning

the act of deserting or walking away from a person or property

Example

The abandonment of a child is a crime punishable by law.

Adultery n.

Meaning

voluntary sexual intercourse by a married person outside his or her marriage

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

alterare ➡️ to alter

Example

Adultery is grounds for divorce.

Alimony n.

Meaning

Money paid by one spouse to an ex-spouse by order of a judge

Derived from

alere ➡️ to nourish

-monia ➡️ suffix signifying action, state, condition

Example

The term alimony is gradually being replaced by the terms maintenance and support.

Annulment n.

Meaning

a court or church order stating that a marriage never existed

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

nullum ➡️ nothing, none

Example

When Betty learned that her husband lied about his felonious past, she was granted an annulment.

Bigamy n.

Meaning

the crime of marrying someone while still married to another

Derived from

bi- ➡️ double

gamos (Greek) ➡️ marrying

Example

Men who commit bigamy lead a double life. Bigamy is a crime punishable by law.

Custody n.

Meaning

(1)the legal right to look after a child

(2)legal confinement, such as protective custody of a person, as well as arrest and imprisonment

Derived from

custodia (Latin) ➡️ guarding, watching, keeping

Example

If a divorcing couple has children, a judge rules on which parent gets primary custody of the children.

Prenuptial agreement

Meaning

a contact between two people who plan to get married that spells out how their property would be divided in case of a divorce or the death of the spouse

Derived from

pre ➡️ before

nuptial ➡️ of or pertaining to marriage or the wedding ceremony

Example

A prenuptial agreement can forestall disputes and lawsuits, especially if there are children from previous marriages.

Separation n.

Meaning

a large agreement between married couples who no longer want to live together but are not divorced

Derived from

se- ➡️ apart

parare ➡️ make ready, prepare

Example

A legal separation agreement allows a husband and a wife to lead independent lives.

Money-related Words

Abscond v.

Meaning

to run away, escape with stolen money or valuables

Derived from

ab(s)- ➡️ away

com ➡️ together

dere ➡️ put

Example

The old woman feared that her maid might abscond with her diamond necklace.

Embezzle v.

Meaning

to steal money or property left in one’s care

Derived from

embesiler (Anglo-French) ➡️ to steal, cause to disappear

Example

The girl’s guardian tried to embezzle her inheritance.

Foreclose v.

Meaning

to take and sell a property to pay for a debt

Derived from

fors ➡️ out

clore ➡️ to shut

Example

If you fail to make your mortgage payment, the bank can foreclose on your house.

Forgery n.

Meaning

the crime of making copies of valuable documents or works of art in order to make money by selling them

Derived from

forge

-ery ➡️ noun suffix signifying “place for, art of, condition of, quantity of”

Example

When Jim withdrew money from the bank by signing his uncle’s name, he was arrested for forgery.

Wills and Estates

Codicil n.

Meaning

a change or explanation added to a will

Derived from

codicillus (Latin) ➡️ a short writing

Example

A codicil must be written as a formal, legal document.

Executor n.

Meaning

the person named in a will who will comply with the wishes of the decedent

Example

John was named as the executor of his uncle’s will.

Holographic will

Meaning

a hand written will

Derived from

holos ➡️ whole

graphos ➡️ written

Example

In some states, a holographic will is not valid.

Intestate adj.

Meaning

dying without a legal will

Derived from

in- ➡️ negative prefix

testari ➡️ make a will, bear witness

Example

When a person dies intestate, there may be a problem settling the estate.

Accidents and Lawsuits

Arbitrate v.

Meaning

to settle an argument or quarrel

Example

A judge may arbitrate a dispute between neighbors.

Bona fide adj.

Meaning

to speak with good intentions, without deceit

Example

Ignoring his mother’s bona fide warning, Tommy fell when he tried to cross the icy street.

Common carrier

Meaning

a ==business== that charges for transporting passengers, goods, or messages

Example

When a taxi driver slammed the door on Betty’s hand, breaking her fingers, both the driver and the common carrier had to pay for her injuries and suffering. (莫名想起”滴滴打车”)

Damages n.

Meaning

the amount of money that a defendant may be ordered to pay as compensation for any harm or loss

Example

When sparks from Dan’s bonfire set his neighbor’s roof on fire, he had to pay for damages to cover the cost of a new roof.

Trespass v.

Meaning

to enter a building or other property illegally

Derived from

tres- ➡️ trans- ➡️ across

passer ➡️ go by, pass

Example

Sam warned his son not to trespass; that is, not to walk across their neighbor’s field without permission.

Victims and Crime

Assault n.

Meaning

a violent threat or attempt to do bodily harm

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

saltus (Latin) ➡️ a leap

Example

His verbal assault made her run from the room to call the police.

Assault and battery

Meaning

violently hitting someone or knocking the person down

Example

The man was arrested for assault and battery after he hit his wife.

Aggravated assault

Meaning

a serious physical attack or an attack with a deadly weapon, especially with an intent to commit a crime.

Example

When the robber hit the clerk with his gun, he committed aggravated assault.

Felony n.

Meaning

a serious crime such as murder, rape, or arson

Example

If you commit a felony, you may be imprisoned for many years.

Misdemeanor n.

Meaning

a less serious crime than a felony

Derived from

mis- ➡️ wrong

de ➡️ completely

mener ➡️ to lead, direct

Example

Bob had to pay a fine and serve a short prison term for the misdemeanor of vandalism

Court-related Terms

Arraignment n.

Meaning

a court hearing during which the accused answers the charge made against him or her

Example

During his arraignment, the felon said he was not guilty.

Commutation n.

Meaning

a reduction in a convicted criminal’s sentence

Example

The commutation allowed the pregnant drug dealer to be out of prison before her baby was born.

Deposition n.

Meaning

a sworn statement; testimony obtained under oath before a trial

Example

A woman who saw the car accident gave a deposition.

Expunge v.

Meaning

to erase completely; to destroy

Derived from

ex- ➡️ out

pungere ➡️ to prick, stab

Example

After a retrial, the judge was willing to expunge the charge from Bill’s criminal record.
