《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter One

发表于 2019-08-12 | 更新于 2019-08-13 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Workplace Words

arduous adj.

originally “high steep”
meaning:exceedingly difficult or challenging

echelon n.

originally”step-like arrangement of troops”
meaning: level of command
eg. The company’s upper echelon gave their approval

egalitarian n

originally “equality”
meaning:believing in fairness and equality
eg. An egalitarian boss treats all her employees fairly and doesn’t show favoritism.

inept adj.

originally ‘unsuitable’
meaning: awkward, inefficient, unskilled

inundate v.

originally
in- either in or on
undare to overflow
meaning: to flood; to overwhelm
eg. Before long, the amount of daily mail would inundate the young secretary.

negligence n.

originally”to neglect”
meaning: carelessness, neglect

More workplace words

meticulous adj.

originally”fear”
meaning: giving exceedingly careful attention to detail
eg. His job in the laboratory required meticulous performance.

tedium n.

originally”to be dull”
meaning: tiredness, boredom
eg. The tedium of her word was wearing her down.

temerity n.

originally”rashly”
meaning: rash, bold behavior
eg. He had the temerity to ask for a raise after failing to complete his assignment.

Shopping vocabulary

apparel n.

originally”to attire in proper clothing”
meaning: clothing especially unusual or formal clothes.

epicure n.

originally”epicouros”
meaning: a person with discriminating taste for food and wine
eg. He was an epicure and favored only the finest French wines.

fetid adj.

originally”have a bad smell, stink”
meaning: unpleasant odor; smelly
eg. The fetid contents of the Dumpster behind the grocery store revolted shoppers.

haute couture n.

from French
meaning: high fashion
eg. She shopped only in boutiques that were haute couture.

memorabilia n.

originally”worthy of being remembered”
meaning: things worthy of remembrance
eg. Antique stores memorabilia.

Banking and Business Words

amortize v.

originally”extinguish a debt”
meaning: to settle a debt by means of installment payments
eg. It took him twenty years to amortize the mortgage on his house.

collateral n.

originally”accompanying”
meaning: money or property pledged as security to ensure repayment of a loan.
eg. The bank loan was denied because the man didn’t have the necessary collateral.

encumbrance n.

meaning: anything that lessens the value of real estate or hinders the sale of property
eg. The mortgage was an encumbrance that hindered the sale of his farm.

fiduciary n.

originally”to trust”
meaning: a person or a bank that has a duty to act for the benefit of another person.
eg. When the child’s parents died, the court appointed a fiduciary.

garnishment n.

(1)meaning: a court order allowing a creditor to collect money or property in payment of a debt.
eg.When Jim failed to pay child support, he was served with a garnishment on behalf of his ex-wife.
(2)meaning: a decoration
eg. Parsley is widely used as a food garnishment.

Talking with friends

colloquial adj.

originally:
com- together
loqui to speak
meaning: characteristic of written or spoken communication that is breezy of informal.
eg.Conversation among friends is generally colloquial.

conviviality n.

meaning: the enjoyment of a friendly gathering to feast and drink and socialize
eg. The festive conviviality of the dinner party appealed to him.

frivolity n.

meaning: lighthearted, silly behavior
eg. The dean disapproved of the students’ frivolity after the game.

jocular adj.

originally”funny, comic”
meaning: joking, witty, amusing
eg. His jocular account of this adventure was very entertaining.

libation n.

originally”to pour, drop”
meaning: The act of pouring out wine, or other liquid
eg. The host offered wine as a libation to his friends.

Talking at Home

appellation n.

originally”name given to a person, thing, or class”
meaning: a name or title
eg. The appellation her brother used to tease Nancy made her angry.

approbation n.

originally”approve”
meaning: praise; approval

decorum n.

originally”fit, proper”
meaning: good social behavior; dignified conduct
eg. In public their children behaved with decorum.

disparagement n.

meaning: verbal abuse
eg. Parental disparagement can seriously damage a child’s self-esteem.

filial adj.

originally”of a son or daughter”
meaning: relating to a child’s relationship with his or her parents
eg. It was his filial duty to honor his parents.
