《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Two

发表于 2019-08-13 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Words with V

Vacuous adj.

Meaning

Lacking substance
or
that which is empty

Derived from

vacuus (Latin) ➡️ empty, void, free

Example

His speech was a vacuous repetition of old slogans.

Vehement adj.

Meaning

strongly felt
or
highly emotional
or
forceful

Derived from

vehe- ➡️ lacking, wanting

mens ➡️ mind

Example

She was vehement in her battle to rescue homeless animals.

Vestige n.

Meaning

Something thais is no longer common
or
something that is lost

Derived from

vestige (French) ➡️ a mark, trace, sigh

Example

The last vestige of the wrecked boat was swept away by the tide.

Vicarious adj.

Meaming

feeling that arise from experience of others rather than one’s own experience

Example

Violent movies gave the boys a vicarious thrill.

Voracious adj.

Meaning

greedily hungry for food or other things that give pleasure

Derived from

voro- ➡️ to devour

Example

Jane, a voracious reader, borrowed stacks of books from the library every week.

Words with T and Th

Tantamount adj.

Meaning

equal to, as in value or significance

Derived from

tant ➡️ as much

amonter ➡️ amount to, go up

Example

When he gave her the engagement ring, it was tantamount to a marriage proposal.

Technocracy n.

Meaning

Government by engineers, technicians, or other highly skilled members of society

Derived from

techno- ➡️ technical, technology

kratia (Greek) ➡️ -cracy ➡️ rule of

Example

The theory of technocracy was greatly popularized in the 1930s.

theocentric adj.

Meaning

placing God in the center of a belief

Derived from

theo- ➡️ God

-centric ➡️ having a center

Example

The theocentric sermon of the preacher inspired his congregation

Thespian n.

Meaning

an actor

Example

He was a fine Shakespearian thespian.

Tome n.

Meaning

a thick, heavy book, usually a scholarly work

Derived from

tom- ➡️ to cut

Example

The professor spent many years writing his tome on evolution.

More words

Iniquity n.

Meaning

injustice
or
immoral behavior

Derived from

in- ➡️ negative prefix
æquus➡️ just, equal

Example

It was an iniquity to sentence the innocent man.

Innocuous adj.

Meaning

harmless
or
bland
or
lacking drama

Derived from

in- ➡️ negative prefix

nocuus ➡️ hurtful

Example

Labeled as a drama, the play was so innocuous it closed after the first performance.

Obsequious adj.

Meaning

servile
or
submissive in order to please superiors

Derived from

ob- ➡️ after

sequ- ➡️ to follow

Example

The obsequious employee failed to attain the favors he sought.

Penultimate adj.

Meaning

next to the last

Derived from

paene ➡️ almost

ultimus ➡️ final

Example

The separation was penultimate to the breakup of the marriage.

Rudimentary adj.

Meaning

Basic
or
the first slight beginning of something

Derived from

rudis ➡️ unlearned, untrained
-ary ➡️ adjective suffix

Example

The piano lesson began with rudimentary finger exercises.

Final words

Abdicate v.

Meaning

to give up
or
to step down from a high position

Derived from

ab- ➡️ away

dicere ➡️ to speak, to say

Example

There are times when a ruler must abdicate to save his life.

Aberration n.

Meaning

a deviation from the right course or standard

Derived from

ab- ➡️ away

errare ➡️ to wander

Example

Tom’s tantrum was an aberration from his usual compliant behavior.

Cartel n.

Meaning

an organization seeking to control a market, or a political group united for a particular cause.

Example

The oil cartel controls our gasoline prices.

Despotism n.

Meaning

a dictatorship

Derived from

dem- ➡️ house or household

poti- ➡️ powerful, lord

-ism ➡️ noun suffix

Example

The people suffered under the monarch’s despotism.

Serendipity n.

Meaning

a fortunate coincidence

Example

Finding an expensive new dress at the consignment shop was pure serendipity.
