《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Three

发表于 2019-08-14 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Grammar Vocabulary

Connotation n.

Meaning

an idea that gives the meaning of a word and also what it implies

Derived from

con- ➡️ together
notare ➡️ to mark

Example

The connotation of the word refuse is less negative than that of the word reject.

Denotation n.

Meaning

the meaning of a word as found in a dictionary

Derived from

de- ➡️ completely

notare ➡️ to mark

Example

The denotation of the word elation is happiness.

Etymology n.

Meaning

the history of words
or
the study of word origins

Derived from

etymos ➡️ true

logos ➡️ word

Example

The etymology of the English language is a fascinating subject.

* Semantics n.

Meaning

the meaning of something, especially words

Derived from

semasia ➡️ signification, meaning

Example

The semantics of a student’s vocabulary test were incorrect.

Syntax n.

Meaning

the part of grammar that deals with the arrangement and relationship of words in a sentence

Derived from

syn- ➡️ together

tassein ➡️ arrange

Example

According to syntax, the subject of a sentence comes before the verb.

Past, Present and Future

Advent n.

Meaning

a first or new appearance

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

venire ➡️ to come

Example

The advent of spring lifted her spirits.

Antiquity n.

Meaning

ancient times

Example

The museum of antiquity contains relics of life in the Middle Ages.

Contemporary adj.

Meaning

existing at the present time

Derived from

con- ➡️ with

tempus ➡️ time

Example

She enjoyed reading contemporary literature.

Eventuality n.

Meaning

something that may happen

Derived from

event- ➡️ to come out, happen, result

-ity ➡️ noun suffix

Example

In the eventuality of a merger, I may lose my job.

Forebode v.

Meaning

predict
or
foretell

Derived from

fore- ➡️ before in time

bode ➡️ announce

Example

The dark clouds forebode a storm.

Imminent adj.

Meaning

something ready to happen, usually evil or bad

Derived from

im- ➡️ in- ➡️ into

minere ➡️ jut out

Example

After the attack, war was imminent.

Modernism n.

Meaning

a practice or usage particular to modern times

Example

Modernism as a movement to adopt religion to modern thought appealed to him.

Obsolete adj.

Meaning

outmoded
or
no longer in use

Derived from

ob- ➡️ away

solere ➡️ to be used to

Example

Slang words used in the twenties are now obsolete.

Posterior adj.

Meaning

later in time

Derived from

post- ➡️ after

Example

The accident was posterior to his drinking too much beer.

Retrospection n.

Meaning

the act of recalling the past

Derived from

retro- ➡️ back

specere ➡️ look at

Example

Talking about his youth, the old man indulged in retrospection.

Possession Vocabulary

Acquisition n.

Meaning

the act of obtaining or gaining possession of something

Derived from

ad- ➡️ extra

quarere ➡️ to seek to obtain

Example

His art collection was a valuable acquisition.

Appropriate v.

Meaning

to take possession of

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

propriare ➡️ take as one’s own

Example

The bank will appropriate my car if I don’t make my payments on time.

Appurtenance n.

Meaning

something belonging to another thing of greater importance

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

pertinere (Latin) ➡️ belong to

Example

The gazebo was an appurtenance to her property.

Ownership n.

Meaning

legal possession

Example

No one questioned her ownership of the estate.

Proprietor n.

Meaning

the owner or manager of a business establishment

Example

The pub’s proprietor was a cheerful man.

Words that imply subordination

Hoi Polloi n. (From Greek)

Meaning

masses
or
general population

Example

The aristocracy felt superior to the hoi polloi.

Maladroit adj.

Meaning

awkward
or
inept
or
unskilled
or
of low status

Derived from

mal- ➡️ bad

adroit ➡️ rightly

Example

The maladroit maid worked hard to learn new skills and to raise her station in life.

Servitude n.

Meaning

a condition of bondage
or
compulsory labor forced upon prisoners

Derived from

servus (Latin) ➡️ a slave

Example

The felon’s servitude lasted a year.

Subjugate v.

Meaning

to dominate
or
to bend to one’s will

Derived from

sub- ➡️ under

ingum ➡️ yoke

Example

The dictator aimed to subjugate his people.

Subservient adj.

Meaning

submissive
or
humble

Derived from

sub- ➡️ under

servire ➡️ serve

Example

The master expected his slaved to be subservient.

Language-Related Vocabulary

Lexicon n.

Meaning

vocabulary of a language
or
a dictionary

Derived from

leg- ➡️ to collect, gather

Example

The law student used a Latin lexicon.

Linguist n.

Meaning

one skilled in languages

Derived from

lingua (Latin) ➡️ language, tongue

Example

My uncle is a linguist.He speaks seven languages.

Morphology n.

Meaning

a part of grammar that deals with word formation

Derived from

morpho- ➡️ shape

-logy ➡️ study

Example

The study of morphology taught her the structure and origin of words.

Philology n.

Meaning

the scientific study of languages

Derived from

philo- ➡️ loving

logos ➡️ word, speech

Example

He was a professor of philology at Cambridge

Vernacular n.

Meaning

a spoken form of language
or
common everyday speech

Derived from

verna (Latin) ➡️ native

Example

It took time for her to learn the vernacular French spoken in a village outside of Paris.
