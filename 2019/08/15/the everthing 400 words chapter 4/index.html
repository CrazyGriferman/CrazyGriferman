《The Everything Build Your Vocabulary Book》 Chapter Four

发表于 2019-08-15 | 更新于 2019-08-16 | 分类于 English-Learning | 评论数： | 阅读次数：

Words that are related to illiteracy

Benighted adj.

Meaning

ignorant
or
unenlightened

Example

The Dark Ages was a benighted era of superstition and fear.

Impercipient adj.

Meaning

imperceptive
or
unaware

Example

He was impercipient of her obvious distress.

Inerudite adj.

Meaning

unlearned

Derived from

in- ➡️ negative prefix

ex- ➡️ out

rudis ➡️ unskilled

Example

The teacher was dismayed at the number of inerudite boys in her class.

Nescience n.

Meaning

ignorance

Derived from

ne- ➡️ not

scire ➡️ to know

Example

He was appalled by the nescience of his coworkers.

Unschooled adj

Meaning

uneducated

Derived from

un- ➡️ not

school

Example

Her aim was to teach the unschooled children in her village.

Contraction Vocabulary

Cohesion n.

Meaning

the action of sticking something tightly together
or
a union

Derived from

co- ➡️ together

haerere ➡️ to adhere, stick

Example

Glue was used to form a strong cohesion between the broken parts of the plate, mending it.

Consolidate v.

Meaning

to bring together; to unite

Derived from

con- ➡️ together

solidare ➡️ to make solid

Example

We need to consolidate our incomes.

Encompassment n.

Meaning

the act of inclusion, of bringing into a circle

Derived from

en- ➡️ make, put in

compass

Example

The brick wall served as an encompassment of his estate.

Incorporate v.

Meaning

to combine
or
to form one body

Derived from

in- ➡️ into

corpus ➡️ body

Example

They decided to incorporate their holdings into one corporation.

Integration n.

Meaning

the act of bringing something into one harmonious unit

Derived from

integrare (Latin) ➡️ make whole

Example

The integration of all races and ethnic groups is a noble undertaking.

Negative-Word Vocabulary

Nonchalant adj.

Meaning

unconcerned
or
indifferent

Derived from

non- ➡️ not
chaloir ➡️ to have concern for

Example

His nonchalant attitude annoyed her.

Noncommittal adj.

Meaning

unwilling to commit oneself to a particular view, course, or the like

Derived from

non- ➡️ not

commit

-al ➡️ adjective suffix

Example

She was very noncommittal when I invited her to the party.

Noncompliance n.

Meaning

disobedience
or
refusal to follow rules

Derived from

non- ➡️ not

comply

-ance ➡️ noun suffix

Example

It’s difficult for parents to deal with teenage noncompliance.

Nonentity n.

Meaning

a thing or person of no importance

Derived from

non- ➡️ not

entity

Example

The boss treated his clerk like a nonentity.

Nonfeasance n.

Meaning

the failure to perform or do something that should be done a duty.

Derived from

non- ➡️ not

facere (Latin) ➡️ to make,do

-ance ➡️ noun suffix

Example

The general who failed to protect his troops was guilty of nonfeasance.

Lay, Lie, and Lie Vocabulary

Ballad n.

Meaning

a lay
or
a song
or
a poem

Derived from

ballare (Latin) ➡️ to dance

Example

She sang an Irish ballad.

Copulation n.

Meaning

the sexual act

Example

Some people consider copulations a carnal sin.

Deception n.

Meaning

the act of deceiving, of misleading
or
fraud

Derived from

de- ➡️ from

capere ➡️ to take 

Example

The con man’s deception left her penniless.

Emplacement n.

Meaning

a location, a position, a placement

Derived from

en- ➡️ in

placer ➡️ place， spot

Example

The soldiers prepared an emplacement for their guns.

Fabrication n.

Meaning

a made-up story
or
an untruth

Example

Her excuse was a fabrication that no one believed.

Prevaricate v.

Meaning

to lie
or
to tell a falsehood

Example

A political candidate may prevaricate in an attempt to defeat his opponent.

Prone adj.

Meaning

lying facedown

Derived from

pronus (Latin) ➡️ bent forward

Example

She slept in a prone position.

Recline v.

Meaning

to lean back
or
to rest
or
to lie down

Derived from

re- ➡️ back, against

clinare (Latin) ➡️ to bend

Example

Let him recline on his sofa.

Recumbent adj.

Meaning

lying down

Derived from

re- ➡️ back

cumbere (Latin) ➡️ to lie down

Example

She stepped over her recumbent dog to go to the kitchen.

Supine adj.

Meaning

lying on one’s back

Example

The baby rolled over to a supine position.

Words of Wisdom

Assiduous adj.

Meaning

diligent
or
persistent

Derived from

ad- ➡️ to

sedere ➡️ to sit

Example

He was an assiduous student.

Cognition n.

Meaning

the process of knowing, understanding, learning

Derived from

com- ➡️ together

gnoscere ➡️ to know

Example

The process of cognition develops rapidly during the first two years of life.

Erudite adj.

Meaning

learned
or
well educated

Derived from

ex- ➡️ out

rudis ➡️ unskilled, rude

Example

The professor was known to be an erudite woman.

Intelligentsia n.

Meaning

a social class of educated intellectuals

Example

The intelligentsia often plays a major role in promoting social and political change.

Omniscience n.

Meaning

the faculty of knowing everything
or
an attribute of God

Derived from

omni- ➡️ all

scientia ➡️ knowledge

Example

Humans do not possess the power of omniscience.
