Astronomical Terms

Ascension n.

Meaning

the rising of a celestial body

Derived from

ascendere ➡️ to mount, go up

Example

They awaited the sun’s ascension.

Asteroid n.

Meaning

one of thousands of small planets chiefly between Mars and Jupiter

Derived from

astro- ➡️ star

-eidos ➡️ form, shape

Example

An asteroid isn’t likely to hit Earth.

Celestial adj.

Meaning

Heavenly

Derived from

calestis ➡️ heavenly

Example

The moon is a celestial body.

Cerulean adj.

Meaning

sky-blue

Derived from

-an ➡️ pertaining to (negative suffix)

caeruleus (Latin) ➡️ blue

Example

They gazed at the clear, cerulean sky.

Constellation n.

Meaning

a group of formation of stars

Derived from

con- ➡️ with

stella ➡️ star

Example

The Big Dipper is a constellation.

Equinox n.

Meaning

a biannual event when the sun crosses the equator and the length of day and night is equal all over the world

Derived from

aquus ➡️ equal

-nox ➡️ night

Example

The vernal (spring) equinox usually occurs on March 21.

Meridian n.

Meaning

a great imaginary circle on Earth’s surface that passes through the North and South poles and at any place between

Derived from

medius ➡️ middle

dies ➡️ day

Example

Astronomers use the meridian line to chart the location of stars.

Planetarium n.

Meaning

an apparatus or model representing the planetary system

Derived from

planet

arium (Latin) ➡️ a place for

Example

The planetarium was housed in a building with a domed ceiling.

Scientific Terms

Artifact n.

Meaning

any object or ornament produced by humans

Derived from

arte (Latin) ➡️ by skill

facere (Latin) ➡️ to make

Example

Archeologists seek prehistoric artifacts.

Eolith n.

Meaning

a crudely chipped stone artifact, such as a flint, from the beginning of human culture

Derived from

eo- ➡️ earliest

Example

An eolith indicates a human being’s first attempt at making a tool out of stone.

Fossil n.

Meaning

An ancient preserved impression or remain of a plant or animal

Derived from

fossilis (Latin) ➡️ dug up

Example

Dinosaur bones and skeletons are fossils.

Homo sapiens

Meaning

the modern species of humans

Derived from

homo ➡️ man

sapiens ➡️ wise, intelligent

Paleolithic Period

Meaning

also known as the Old Stone Age; a subdivision of the Stone Age

Derived from

paleo- ➡️ ancient, early

lithos (Greek) ➡️ stone

Example

Neanderthal and the Cro-Magnon early humans belonged to the Paleolithic Period.

Invisible World

Immunize v.

Meaning

to protect from disease by the use of a vaccine or other method

Example

It is important to immunize infants against diphtheria and other childhood diseases.

Microbiology n.

Meaning

deals with microorganisms

Derived from

micro- ➡️ small

biology

Example

Researchers in microbiology have made significant scientific and medical discoveries.

Weather Vocabulary

Cirrus n.

Meaning

a white, wispy cloud

Derived from

cirrus (Latin) ➡️ a lock of hair

Example

The cirrus cloud floated across the sky in a feather-shaped band.

Cumulus n.

Meaning

massive, dense clouds, seen in fair weather

Derived from

cumulus (Latin) ➡️ heap

Example

Against the azure sky, the white cumulus clouds looked like mounds of whipped cream on a blue plate.
