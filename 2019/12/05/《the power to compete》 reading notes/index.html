P2~3

The causes of the stagnation of the Japanese economy

  1. Industrial policy lead by the government bureaucracy, which doesn’t do anything except stifle competitiveness in the private sector.

  2. The lack of sufficient managerial power

P4~5

“Japan Again” ➡️ five factors

  1. Efficiency of the country (increased management efficiency of government)
  2. Innovation capability (IT autobahn concept)
  3. Operation capability (labor market)
  4. Global expansion and market capability
  5. Brand power

P5

IT autobahn concept about the communication infrastructure (like the German autobahn, which has no tolls or speed limits. The idea calls for the nationalization of IT infrastructure and the creation of the world’s most advanced free, and open communication environment)

P12

P13

How Samsung is developing its globalization strategy

Every year, they send hundreds of employees to dozens of countries around the world.(only South Korean employees?)

They have a system of regional specialists within the country and they have their employees stay abroad for a year.

P15

Schumpeter’s economy theory:
Innovation itself was the source of profit.

P17

Price competition could lead to the end of profits.As competition continued, the excess rate of profit would disappear.

James Duesenbeery coined the term brand ➡️ “The demonstration effect”

P18

Consumption propensity means the ratio of income that people are willing to spend on consumption.

When people buy products, they don’t just think about whether the product is good or bad, but they also consider the demonstration effect.

P19

The multiplier effect ➡️ you are referring to times when investment increases and the resulting increase in income is greater than the increased investment amount.

P21

Communications infrastructure

P25

Bad companies should fail (absolutely right)

Creative destruction. If you mean to create, you can’t avoid destruction.

P27

Economic growth is the continuous development of innovations arising from combinations of new connections.

The role and purpose of the government should extend no further than the creation of platforms for industry.

Promote the IT autobahn concept to construct a faster and cheaper communications infrastructure throughout the world.

P30

Japan’s power to operate is so low because of the underutilization of IT and the excessive costs of the public sector.

The problem of Japanese employment

  1. The lack of balance (old and young workers) suboptimal
  2. Employment rigidity

P31

In Japan, once you hire employees, you cannot let them go

P36

A withe-collar exemption:
a system where instead of providing white-collar workers with an executive allowance, they are made exempt from a variety of regulations, including working hours system.

P40

The abolishment of the lifetime employment is essential for Japan to regain its competitiveness.

Mandatory retirement age

P41

In US, it is illegal to even ask them their age when employing people.

Marxian economic theory ➡️ the value of a good is determined by the amount human labor it involves.

P42

Workforce fluidity will prompt to foster innovation and create new fields of business without first creating workforce fluidity.

P48

Nonnative English is often called Globish

P53

P57

If there is force behind fiscal policy, it will cause inflation. (historical experience)

P59

Japan used to be in the period of deflation (Does it come across it now?)

Whether interest rates were decided based on the stock of currency in existence or based on the flows of capital as well as supply and demand and so on.

P62

Labor unions

P63

At 3 percent to 5 percent, expectations of inflation will rise swiftly, inflation will lead to more inflation, and then once we pass a certain time, all will accelerate.

Theory of inflation targeting. (encourage inflation by setting a certain target for inflation)

  1. Problem: determine what percentage the inflation rate should be sit at. (vicious cycle: prices go up when wages go up and wages go up when prices go up)

  2. Problem: how we think about inflation after a period of continued deflation

P64

If productivity rises, even if monetary easing is done, there should be a damper on the rise in prices.

If there was an explosive improvement in productivity, there would be an increase in unemployment.

⭐️ Monetary easing is the key to achieving inflation targets.

P65

Expectations toward inflation are not something that changes gradually over a month or so.

The only way to suppress a rise in interest rates is through credit rationing.

P66

Tax revenue can increase as a result of a decrease in tax rates.

P68

A centralized governmental system is high-cost system.

Absorb the debt through mild inflation (around 2 percent per year)

P69

If the economy improves while increasing debts, it is certainly dangerous.

P71

P74

“From concrete to people”

P75

The independence of the Bank of Japan is indispensable to fiscal policy

Invest in communications infrastructure and education can produce a large multiplier effect

P79

China’s corporate income tax rate is 25 percent

P81

A Laffer curve: a graph showing that although tax revenues rise after tax rates are hiked, tax revenues conversely decline once the tax rate hits a certain number.

P82

The high price of the utility gas
reason:

  1. Regionanl monopolies
  2. Liquefied natural gas (LNG) prices are linked to the price of oil

Japan’s off-take agreements with oil-producing countries include a clause where the price of LNG increases whenever the price of oil rises. Only Japan is subject to this linkage. (a kind of long-term agreement to secure a stable supply of oil)

P83

E-signatures

P87

The Japanese disease is the illusion that anything led by bureaucrats will go well and that the bureaucrats will save us if something goes wrong.

Reason to cause :
P88 the protectionist policy of the METI（对日企过度保护，在国际上的竞争力变低）

P88

Instead of handing out subsidies, I think it would be more beneficial if we applied tax incentives to R&D investments instead.

P89

Fundamental R&D should be led by the government.

The other key point to mid- to long-term R&D is that it shouldn’t be done by Japanese nationals alone.

P96

What conditions are needed for a bureaucracy to succeed?

  1. Clear policy targets (which match the needs of the country)

P98

Singapore

  1. Political- appointee system (where top political leadership appoint the top bureaucrats)
  2. Use separate personnel evaluation systems for high-ranking officials and for local officials

P99

An archaic Galapagos strategy: companies only care about their home market and are reluctant to do anything else.

P101

Japan’s bureaucracy should deregulate to the greatest extent possible and align Japan with international standards

P102

‘Every dog is a lion at home.’

The infallibility of the bureaucracy

P103

Create a think tank （智囊团？）

P105

We need to create a framework through which talented human resources (corporate executives) can work for the bureaucracy over five or ten years and then return to the private sector.

We should make all vice ministers political appointees.

P111

Moratorium policies that extend the life of companies that should be left to fail is a major problem created by Japan’s bureaucracy.

Fully implement IT solutions in government to reduce the number of public employees by half

The annual cost of government services shouldn’t exceed 20 percent of the GDP

P112

Create tax credits for investments in R&D

Introduce a system to make the director and vice minister positions of ministries and government agencies into political appointments.

Create a think tank （智囊团？）

P115

P116

The reason why Japanese don’t study abroad

  1. They will miss out on opportunities to look for work here

  2. The lack of funding for studies abroad

  3. It’s too comfortable to continue living in Japan

journalists (become) opinion leaders

P127

From the perspective of improving Japanese society as a whole, journalists must have their own ,independent opinions.

And to that end, journalists must all function as researchers in the widest sense of that word.

P128

The business model of broadcasting in Japan is tied to the monopolization of broadcasting signals.

P131

There is a need for high-level journalism to continuously publish critical reporting. (separate journalism from politicians, the bureaucracy …)

P137

Eliminate the prefectural broadcasting license and press club systems

Set a KPI calling for an increase in the number of students studying abroad by the tens of thousands.

P139

“to educate” ➡️ to bring out potential

P140

A top-down educational structure

Asking people to obey your instructions is the same as telling them not to think.

P151

What is really important is that people have the ability to think about that is most important to them.

P151

Good teaching is not about making people remember what you want to teach. It should actually be thought of as motivation management. It is about supporting the drive people have to learn.

P162

Social learning —— Participants undertake interactive studies and join group discussions through the Internet, and they improve their skills this way.

P168

Create a peer-to peer evaluation system for teachers

Make Tofel a part of university entrance examinations

P174

Keynesian economics:

If you want to increase, employment, all you need to do is stimulate the economy through such means as increasing investment.

Marginal efficiency of capital:

recommend that governments improve the investment environment and implement policies that enhance investment efficiency

P176

The demonstration effect:

People see other people wearing fashionable clothes, and they begin to think that they want to by those clothes.

Ratchet effect:

Once people have experienced or purchased something nice, they will continue to spend the same amount of money for it if they can receive the same experience or product.

P176

Relative income hypothesis:

The income of a single person is not determined independently of other variables; it is actually affected by the relative size of other people’s incomes, and it rises and falls in relation to those other income levels.

P190

Comparative advantage

Because each country specializes in what it excels at, the promotion of trade can make one plus one equal three.

P195

Increase the number of foreign firms with a head office in Japan

Eliminate the foreign player restrictions in professional sports and increase the number of foreign athletes in Japan

P200

Priority ➡️ the competitiveness of the state platform

P205

We must first create an attractive country, a platform, if we are never going to draw superior minds to Japan and see future in which many more innovative experiments are being done here.

Create an economic platform that positions Japan as a country attractive to foreign nationals. (Boys, be ambitious !)
